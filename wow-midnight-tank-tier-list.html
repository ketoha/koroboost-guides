WoW Midnight Tank Tier List

Season 1 tank rankings for all content. Last updated March 2026.

Tanking in Midnight is a different beast than The War Within. The eight new dungeons hit harder than anything in Season 4, and the Sunwell Reclaimed raid has tank-swap mechanics on six of nine bosses. Your choice of tank spec affects your group's success more than any other role this season. Here is the breakdown after thousands of runs across our Mythic+ carry team.

What Makes a Good Tank in Midnight

Three factors matter: survivability (can you live through Void Eruption hits that deal 2.5M raw damage), DPS contribution (tanks that do more damage help time keys and shorten raid encounters), and route flexibility (your CC toolkit determines what pulls you can make in M+). Survivability is weighted heaviest because a dead tank is a wipe.

S-Tier

S Tier

Blood Death Knight

Blood DK is the best tank in Midnight by a meaningful margin. Death Strike healing scales with damage taken, and Midnight dungeons hit HARD, which paradoxically makes Blood DK stronger. On Fortified +15 pulls in the Sunstrider Crypts gauntlet, Blood DK can self-heal 18-20M HP over a 30-second pull without external healing. The new Crimson Scourge talent interacts with the Midnight tier set to reset Dancing Rune Weapon every 40 seconds, giving them near-permanent parry uptime. Their DPS is competitive too, averaging 1.4M on trash in +15s. The only weakness is mobility. You need to plan your pulls around having no gap-closer besides Death's Advance.

Protection Paladin

Prot Paladin is the king of utility. Word of Glory self-healing is strong, Ardent Defender and Guardian of Ancient Kings give you two "I refuse to die" buttons, and Blessing of Freedom trivializes movement mechanics in Thalassian Depths and Elrendar Crossing. Their DPS is the highest of any tank at 1.6M on AoE pulls thanks to Avenger's Shield bouncing to 8 targets with the Midnight talent. In raid, they handle the Kael'thas Reborn tank swap cleanly because Blessing of Protection removes the Flamestrike debuff. The only reason they are not clearly above Blood DK is raw survivability. On massive pulls, they need healer attention where Blood DK does not.

A-Tier

A Tier

Vengeance Demon Hunter

VDH has the best kiting toolkit in the game. Sigil of Misery, Sigil of Chains, and double jump/glide let you kite packs that would flatten other tanks. In Magisters' Terrace, where the Sunfury Magisters apply a stacking debuff that increases damage taken by 5% per stack, VDH can kite for 8 seconds to let it fall off while other tanks have to eat the hits. Fiery Brand makes big hits survivable, and their damage profile is smooth and consistent. Their weakness is sustained tankiness. Between cooldowns, they feel fragile compared to Blood DK or Prot Paladin.

Brewmaster Monk

Brewmaster's stagger mechanic smooths out the massive damage spikes in Midnight perfectly. When a Void Eruption hit lands for 2.5M, Brewmaster takes 1.5M immediately and staggers the rest, giving healers time to react. Celestial Brew is a strong absorb, and their mobility with Roll and Transcendence is excellent. They also bring the 5% physical damage debuff via Mystic Touch, which is valuable if your DPS is melee-heavy. DPS output is average at 1.1M on trash, which holds them back from S-tier.

B-Tier

B Tier

Protection Warrior

Prot Warrior is playable but feels outdated. Shield Block and Ignore Pain are strong defensives, but their uptime is not enough for the sustained damage in Midnight dungeons. Spell Reflection is incredible on specific pulls (it trivializes the Arcane Sentinels in Dawnstar Spire), but it is situational. Ravager does good AoE damage and the new Thunderous Roar talent gives them a useful bleed/slow, but their overall DPS at 1.0M is the lowest of any tank. Shockwave stun is the best tank CC in the game, and it single-handedly keeps Prot Warrior viable. Without it, they would drop to C-tier.

Guardian Druid

Guardian has the HP pool to survive anything, but that is about it. They got 14M+ HP with the Midnight tier set, which is 2M more than any other tank. Ironfur stacking gives them strong physical mitigation, and Frenzied Regeneration heals for a fixed percentage, which scales well with their huge HP pool. The problems are DPS (900K on trash, dead last) and utility (no interrupt outside of Skull Bash, limited CC). In M+ routes that require precise control of mobs, Guardian Druid forces suboptimal pathing. In raid, they are actually decent because raw survivability matters more and their Stampeding Roar is valuable for movement mechanics.

Tank Tips for Midnight M+

  • Always pull with your back against a wall in Sunstrider Crypts. The Crypt Stalkers do a knockback that will send you into the next pack.
  • In Elrendar Crossing, the river sections slow you by 50%. Plan your route to minimize river crossings or use movement abilities.
  • The Void Corruption affix at +12 and above ticks every 3 seconds. As a tank, you need to use a minor defensive for it on top of whatever you are using for the trash.
  • In Dawnstar Spire, the elevator boss (Sunsworn Commander Tyralion) requires precise positioning on a small platform. Practice the pull on a lower key first.
  • Always interrupt Arcane Volley in Magisters' Terrace. If it goes off, it does 1.8M to the entire group and the healer cannot recover.

Final Thoughts

Blood DK and Prot Paladin are head and shoulders above the rest. If you are serious about pushing +15s or progressing Mythic Sunwell Reclaimed, play one of those two. VDH and Brewmaster are strong in the right hands but have higher skill floors. Prot Warrior and Guardian Druid are viable for Heroic raid and keys up to +12 but will struggle beyond that without exceptional play.
