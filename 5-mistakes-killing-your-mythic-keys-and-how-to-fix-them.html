5 Mistakes Killing Your Mythic+ Keys (And How to Fix Them)

A guide by Koroboost

You're in a +7 Priory of the Sacred Flame. You've got decent gear, your DPS is respectable, but somehow you keep timing out. Not because the healer's bad or the tank's undergeared—but because something keeps going wrong that you can't quite identify.

This is a mechanically tight season. Mistakes that were forgivable in The War Within are now costing you full seconds, which turns into failed timers. Here are the five mistakes I see constantly that are actually fixable.

Mistake #1: Not Understanding Your Route (Timer Tax)

This one's stupid simple but people skip it constantly. They get into a key and just go. They pull random packs, they skip things, they spend 10 minutes trying to figure out the optimal path while the clock's ticking.

What's actually happening: Every dungeon has a mathematically optimal route. You pull specific packs in specific order to minimize walking time and maximize the density of mobs you're facing. If you're running random pulls, you're losing 30-60 seconds per instance just from inefficiency.

The fix:

  • Learn one route. Not all routes. One. Priory has different variations, but pick the most consistent one and run it ten times.
  • Walk the route before the dungeon once. Seriously. You can skip to a specific difficulty and just run the path without fighting anything.
  • Use Mythic Dungeon Tools (web browser tool, free). It shows you the exact pulls pros use.
  • Pull packs that are together. Don't pull scattered mobs from different rooms.

Specific example: In Priory, the trash before the second boss doesn't need all of it. Knock out the high-damage zealots and the shield priests. Skip the weaker followers. That's 15 seconds back into your timer.

The route makes the difference between a 12-minute dungeon and a 14-minute dungeon. That's timing out versus being comfortable.

Mistake #2: Bad Interrupt Priorities (Letting Casters Run)

You've got a rogue, a DH, a DK, and a priest in party. Someone's always available to interrupt. But nobody's actually watching what needs to be interrupted, so casters just finish their casts and suddenly your healer's taking 40% of their health in damage.

What's actually happening: You're letting interrupt-dependent abilities cast full duration. Some things are fatal if they hit. Some things are just damage. Your group isn't coordinating which is which.

The fix:

  • Identify fatal interrupts before the pull. If you don't know what a caster is doing, ask in party. Example: Priory zealots cast a big holy damage nuke. That's fatal on low-health characters. Interrupt it.
  • Assign interrupts to specific people. "Rogue handle the left caster" is 10x better than "someone interrupt." Now the rogue watches that caster.
  • Let some casts go if they're not dangerous. Your healer doesn't need you burning interrupts on a damage buff cast. Save interrupts for the damage nuke.
  • Communicate if your interrupt is down. You're a rogue on a 15-second interrupt cooldown. If you just used it, say "interrupt down" so your DH can pick up the next one.

Specific example: In Brackenhall Gardens, the druid casters cast a heal. You don't need to interrupt healing. They cast a crowd control ability. Interrupt that. The distinction saves you from wasting utilities.

Mistake #3: Standing in Bad Mechanics (Healer Tax)

Someone stands in a mechanic that does damage. The healer now has to heal them back. That's a GCD wasted. That means the healer can't top off someone else. That cascades into someone else eating mechanics they shouldn't.

What's actually happening: You're not respecting the dungeon. You think you can out-sustain mechanics. You can't. Not in +7s and above.

The fix:

  • Actually move. I know that sounds condescending, but if you're struggling to time keys, this is the issue. You're standing in bad. Stop.
  • Watch the animation cues. Each boss/elite has a tell before the big attack. Priestess in Priory? She casts an animation, then does AOE damage on people in front of her. Move behind her.
  • Predict where bad is. Certain mobs spawn AOE circles. You know AOE circles are coming. Be positioned so you're not in them when they spawn.
  • Accept that standing still is for threat meters, not for keys. Your DPS doesn't matter if you're dead or the healer's out of mana healing you.

Specific example: In Priory, the second boss spawns circles under your feet. You have approximately 1.5 seconds to move before it hits. Move immediately. Don't wait. Definitely don't try to finish your cast.

Mistake #4: Pulling Things You Can't Handle (Threat Management)

Your tank pulled three packs. There's 15 enemies. You're an AOE DPS and you're hitting everything. The tank loses threat on some of the adds. They run at your healer. Your healer takes damage. Your healer dies. Key failed.

What's actually happening: Tank pulled too much, but you pulled threat because you hit faster than the tank could establish threat. Or you pulled something that shouldn't be pulled at all.

The fix:

  • Respect the tank's pull. If the tank pulled three packs, they pulled three packs for a reason. Hit them. Don't panic-AOE.
  • Wait 2-3 seconds for threat to establish. Your tank needs time to get initial threat. Let them. Most threat problems disappear if you give it 2 seconds.
  • Reduce AOE on packs with small adds. If there are shield followers in the pack, your AOE might accidentally pull more adds. Single-target the followers, AOE the main pack.
  • *Know what shouldn't be pulled.* Some enemies are specifically skippable. If your tank isn't pulling something, don't AOE it accidentally. Priory has specific skippable followers. Don't cleave them.

Specific example: In Priory trash, sometimes there are followers healing enemies. The tank might deliberately leave them out of the initial pull, kill everything else, then focus the healer. If you AOE-cleave into the healer, you've wasted their cooldown and complicated the pull. Don't do that.

Mistake #5: Bad Cooldown Management (Forcing Healer to Sit Empty)

Your healer has three defensive cooldowns available. You're about to take a big hit. Instead of using your own defensive cooldown, you just... let it hit and expect your healer to save you.

What's actually happening: Healers have limited resources. Once they're out of cooldowns, they die. If you force them to use cooldowns on preventable damage, they have nothing left when the actual danger happens.

The fix:

  • Use your own defensives first. You have damage mitigation. Warrior shield wall, DH metamorphosis, rogue feint. Use these before you need healer help.
  • Predict big damage. Some attacks hit harder than others. Boss casts a nuke? Pop your defensive. Don't wait until you're at 20% health.
  • Tell your healer what your cooldowns are. "My wall's up" or "I'm defensive heavy on this one" lets your healer plan resource management.
  • *Accept that you will take some damage and that's okay.* The healer doesn't need to keep you topped off 100% of the time. You can sit at 60% health. That's fine. That's normal.

Specific example: Boss is doing a big AOE attack on the whole group. You have a personal cooldown. Use it. Don't make your healer burn a cooldown when you have tools available.

Putting It Together: A Single Pull Example

You're at the first trash pull in Priory. There are three zealots and two followers.

Right way:

  • Tank pulls the three zealots and one follower (skips the second follower).
  • DPS wait 2 seconds for threat.
  • Rogue focuses the left zealot and interrupts their nuke.
  • DH watches middle zealot.
  • When zealots die, finish the follower.
  • Total pull: 30 seconds, no big damage, no healing spent.

Wrong way:

  • Tank pulls zealots, DPS immediately hit all five enemies.
  • Threat's everywhere. Zealots getting interrupted inconsistently.
  • A zealot casts its nuke because rogue was on CD.
  • Healer burns a cooldown.
  • Pull takes 50 seconds. Healer is now vulnerable for the next pull.

See the difference? It's execution. It's not gear. It's not your classes being "weak." It's how you're playing together.

The Real Problem (And The Real Fix)

Most key failures aren't because of one big mistake. They're because of accumulation of small mistakes. You lose 10 seconds here, burn a healer cooldown there, stand in bad over there. That adds up to 2+ minutes by the end of the dungeon, and now you're not timing.

The fix is simple: Be intentional. Know your route. Watch what needs interrupting. Don't stand in bad. Let your tank tank. Use your own cooldowns. Do those five things and your key timing problems mostly disappear.
