Midnight Season 1 Dungeon Tier List: Easiest to Hardest M+ Keys Ranked

A guide by Koroboost

# Midnight Season 1 Dungeon Tier List: Easiest to Hardest M+ Keys Ranked There are five dungeons in Midnight season 1, and they're absolutely not created equal. Some of them are free loot if you know the routes. Others will dumpster your group if anyone's half asleep. Here's the actual tier list based on what's meta right now. ## S-Tier (Easiest): Spirestorm, Shadow Crypt **Spirestorm** is the most braindead dungeon this expansion. The route is short, most packs are non-threatening, and the bosses have laughable mechanics. Literally the dungeon you run when you want free rating points. Even on +15, it's just a matter of not standing in obvious bad. The only remotely scary part is the second boss if your group is low on DPS, but even that's just add management. Kill the adds, don't die. Your healer will have the easiest run of their life. **Shadow Crypt** is a close second. Slightly more annoying packs because the necromancer guys do some damage, but the boss mechanics are still trivial. This is where you take your new player buddies. You can literally carry them and still time the key easily. ## A-Tier: Darkflood Citadel **Darkflood Citadel** is where things start getting slightly spicy. The routes are relatively clean, but some of the trash packs hit harder than they look. If your DPS is bad, you'll start struggling to time at higher levels. The real issue with Darkflood is the boss mechanics. Nothing's super hard, but they're punishing if people don't move. The third boss especially has a mechanic that kills people who don't pay attention. Still very killable though; it's just not a free run like Spirestorm. Affixes matter way more in Darkflood. Raging weeks are annoying. Grievous weeks are miserable. But base dungeon? It's totally fair. ## B-Tier: Stonevault **Stonevault** is basically mid-tier difficulty. It's got some tighter route options, some stun-immune packs that require planning, and fights that have actual mechanics your group needs to understand. The second boss is the skill check in this dungeon. If your healer can't handle the damage, people die. There's not a lot of room to be bad here. This is where you start needing actual coordination. For pushing keys, Stonevault is annoying because the route's locked in pretty tight. No skips, so you're running a lot of trash. But at least the trash is manageable with any comp. It's not like some dungeons where you need specific classes or you're wiping. ## B+ Tier: Ara-Kara, Scroll of Splintering **Ara-Kara** is deceptively spicy. The route itself isn't bad, but the packs do real damage and the bosses have solid mechanics. If your group can execute, it's fine. If people are standing in bad or not interrupting, it falls apart fast. The final boss is legit one of the hardest final bosses in the rotation. It's not mechanically complex, but it hits stupidly hard and requires good awareness. People respecting the mechanics is the difference between a kill and a wipe. **Scroll of Splintering** is similar in that it looks easier than it plays. Trash packs seem manageable until the affixes show up, then suddenly you're getting slapped. The bosses are straightforward, but your group needs to focus. Not a dungeon to autopilot. ## C-Tier (Hardest): Nightmaul, The Galvak Wait, Nightmaul isn't even in Midnight. Scratch that. Let's be real: Ara-Kara is probably the hardest dungeon in Midnight season 1 on pure difficulty, but Scroll of Splintering is the hardest to consistently time because of how tight the routing is. Actually, all five dungeons are pretty playable if your group knows what it's doing. The tier list really comes down to route clarity and affix sensitivity more than base difficulty. ## How Affixes Shake Up The Tier List This is important: affixes change everything. Spirestorm goes from S-tier to A-tier on Raging weeks. Stonevault becomes a nightmare on Grievous/Volcanic weeks. Shadow Crypt is always chill. If you're pushing rating, you need to understand which dungeons get worse on specific affix combinations. That matters way more than the base dungeon difficulty. ## Comp Requirements Per Dungeon **Spirestorm & Shadow Crypt**: Any comp works. Really. Bring whatever. **Darkflood**: You want at least one interrupt source and decent AoE. Otherwise fine. **Stonevault**: Single-target DPS helps because you can't skip everything. But not required if your group is good. **Ara-Kara & Scroll**: You want good defensive CDs and ideally an interrupt. CC is nice on Scroll if you're going for time. This is why the dungeon tier matters. Some dungeons let you get away with suboptimal comps. Others will punish you immediately. ## Best Dungeons For Rating Pushes If you're trying to climb rating without brainmelting yourself: **Spirestorm** is the obvious choice. High success rate, easy mechanics, fast routes. **Shadow Crypt** is the second option. Also super chill, slightly more trash, still very doable. **Darkflood** is actually pretty smooth if you run it a few times. People overestimate the difficulty. Skip Ara-Kara and Scroll if you're trying to push rating. They're fun dungeons, but they're efficiency killer because of how tight the margins are on time. ## Speedrunning Tier For actually speedrunning and blasting keys, the list is different: **Spirestorm** still wins because of how short it is. **Shadow Crypt** is second because you can run past a lot of trash if you have the damage. **Scroll** is actually pretty good for speedrunning because the route is optimized and there's room for skill expression. Darkflood and Ara-Kara aren't great for speedrunning because you have to stop for certain packs and can't optimize much. ## Healer Difficulty Ranking If you're a healer, you care about a different tier list: **Easiest**: Shadow Crypt (nothing hits that hard) **Medium**: Spirestorm, Darkflood (medium ramp, telegraphed damage) **Hard**: Stonevault (consistent pressure), Ara-Kara (bursty damage on second boss) **Hardest**: Scroll of Splintering (the boss fights are sustained high-damage checks) Your healing matters way more than DPS in these dungeons if your group isn't geared. Don't carry undergeared groups into Scroll; you'll burn out. ## Tanking Perspective Tanks don't really have a hard tier list in Midnight because everything's manageable with proper CDs. Shadow Crypt and Spirestorm are baby mode for tanks. Scroll is the most engaging because you can actually pull big and flex routing. Darkflood is annoying because some packs are tight and you need to position carefully. But that's not hard, just tedious. ## Gearing For Each Dungeon Fresh 80 (ilvl 262)? Spirestorm and Shadow Crypt only. Honestly, skip Shadow Crypt until 265+. ilvl 270? All dungeons are playable on +6 or +7. Start running Ara-Kara and Scroll around here. ilvl 280+? Push whatever you want. Comp and knowledge matter way more than gear at this point. ## The Meta Shift At the start of the season, everyone thought Ara-Kara was the hardest. After a month, people realized Scroll is actually harder because of the routing and timer tightness. Give it time and people will find even more optimizations. The dungeon meta will shift based on what affixes are up and what the community figures out for routes. Right now, Spirestorm is the undisputed easiest and fastest gearing dungeon. ## Key Recommendations By Goal **Want fast rating?** Run Spirestorm every single run until you hit your target, then switch. **Want practice?** Run Darkflood and Ara-Kara because they teach you mechanics. **Want to speedrun?** Learn Spirestorm routing and optimize like crazy. **Want variety?** Rotate all five, but skip Scroll and Ara-Kara on weeks where affixes make them obnoxious. ## Learning The Routes Routes matter way more than you think. If you're new to a dungeon, watch a guide first. Spirestorm forgives bad routing. Scroll will kill you if you don't know where packs are. Most people use routes from mainstream dungeon guides, which is fine. Just pick one and stick with it instead of improvising. ## Getting Better At Keys Honestly, the best way to improve at Mythic+ is just running more keys and getting experience with each dungeon. But right now, this is where the dungeons rank based on difficulty, routing, and how much they punish mistakes. Good luck pushing keys. Pick Spirestorm first, get some confidence, then branch out. You'll be timing +15s in no time.
