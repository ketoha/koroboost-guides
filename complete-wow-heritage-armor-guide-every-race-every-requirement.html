Complete WoW Heritage Armor Guide: Every Race, Every Requirement

A guide by Koroboost

Heritage Armor is one of World of Warcraft's best cosmetic rewards. You hit a specific level on a character of a certain race, complete a short questline, and you unlock a full transmog set that's exclusive to that race. The problem is that Blizzard's spread these quests across multiple expansions, multiple level ranges, and multiple time periods. If you're trying to collect all of them, it's confusing fast.

This guide covers every single Heritage Armor set, what you need to get it, and the fastest way to farm them all.

How Heritage Armor Works (The System)

Here's the basic structure:

  • Level requirement: You need a character of a specific race at a specific level.
  • Quest requirement: You return to a starting area or capital city and talk to an NPC.
  • Duration: Most questlines take 10-30 minutes of actual playtime.
  • Reward: A full transmog set (usually 8-10 pieces) that only that race can use.
  • Mog-only: These sets are not gear. They're pure cosmetics. You can transmog them onto your actual gear.

You get one set per race. Your human can't wear orc heritage armor. Your orc can't wear night elf armor. But once you unlock it on one character of that race, all characters of that race can transmog it.

Getting Started (The Fastest Path)

If you want all Heritage Armor sets, here's the strategy:

Step 1: Pick your races in order of leveling speed.

Some classes level way faster than others. Demon Hunters start at 98. Death Knights start at 55. Monks start at 1 but level fast. Plan accordingly.

Step 2: Get them to the required level (varies per race).

More on specific requirements below, but most Heritage Armor questlines happen between level 20-50 or level 50+.

Step 3: Run the questline (15-30 minutes).

Each race's questline is different but they're all solo-able and relatively quick.

Step 4: Transmog it onto your gear.

Go to a transmog NPC and apply the sets to your actual equipment.

Step 5: Move to the next race.

Repeat.

The total time investment for all sets? Roughly 5-7 hours of playtime, depending on how fast you level and how efficient you are with quests.

Every Heritage Armor Set (Complete Breakdown)

Alliance Races

Human Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: Stormwind (Mathias Shaw in SI:7 Headquarters)
  • Time: 15 minutes
  • What it looks like: Classic fantasy warrior gear. Plate armor with blue and gold accents.
  • Best use: Any warrior or paladin. It's clean.

Dwarf Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: Ironforge (Muradin Bronzebeard in Ironforge or Khardros in the Badlands)
  • Time: 15 minutes
  • What it looks like: Heavy plate armor, very dwarf-y (short and bulky, lots of chainmail).
  • Best use: Miners and brewers (and warriors).

Night Elf Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: Darnassus/Rut'theran Village (Sentinel Leyreada or in Teldrassil)
  • Time: 20 minutes
  • What it looks like: Elven design with curves, leather/mail emphasis, teal and purple tones.
  • Best use: Hunters, druids, rogues. It's genuinely pretty.

Gnome Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: Gnomeregan (various NPCs)
  • Time: 20 minutes
  • What it looks like: Tiny gnome tech-wear. It's actually adorable. Leather and cloth with goggles.
  • Best use: Mages, warlocks, engineers.

Draenei Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: The Exodar (Megelon near the bank)
  • Time: 15 minutes
  • What it looks like: Space alien armor with blue crystal accents. Futuristic.
  • Best use: Priests, shamans, paladins. It looks incredible on space-themed characters.

Worgen Heritage Armor

  • Level required: 20+
  • Location: Gilneas (various NPCs, starts in Gilneas City)
  • Time: 25 minutes
  • What it looks like: Gothic, werewolf-themed. Dark leather, lots of furs.
  • Best use: Death knights, warriors, rogues. It's edgy and cool.

Pro Tips for Farming All Sets

1. Use your fastest leveler first. If you have a Demon Hunter, start there. You'll save hours.

2. Use Dragonflight Renown for alternate leveling.

If you're 70+ on other characters, you can convert Renown items to scale down characters. It's not meta but it works.

3. Group up with friends.

You can't do the quests in groups, but having someone to chat with while you level makes it less tedious.

4. Don't stress the "optimal" build.

You're leveling for 20-50 minutes per character. Literally any build works. Use what you enjoy.

5. Do the quests on the character that will use the armor.

You'll get to see how the set looks in actual gameplay immediately.

6. Some sets look better dyed.

Once you have the armor, don't be afraid to use dyes. A lot of these sets pop with the right colors.

Which Sets are Actually Worth Collecting?

Real talk: collect all of them. They're all genuinely cool. But if you're only going for favorites:

  • Best looking: Blood Elf, Dracthyr, Zandalari Troll, Night Elf
  • Most versatile: Worgen, Human, Draenei
  • Funniest: Goblin, Gnome, Mechagon Gnome
  • Most edgy: Undead, Dark Iron Dwarf, Void Elf
  • Most magical: Troll, Nightborne, Void Elf

If you're trying to rank them for "which one should I do first," go with whichever race you have the highest level character of. You'll hit the requirement fastest and unlock your first set immediately.
