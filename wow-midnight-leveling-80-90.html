Midnight Leveling 80-90

Fastest routes and tips for hitting level cap in WoW Midnight. Updated March 2026.

← Back to All Guides

So you've finally hit level 80 in World of Warcraft's Midnight expansion and now you're staring at the grind to 90. Whether this is your main or your tenth alt, getting through those levels efficiently matters -- especially if you want to start raiding, push Mythic+ keys, or just enjoy endgame content faster.

Here's the thing: Midnight's leveling zones are genuinely well-designed, and there are several viable routes depending on your playstyle. Some players love questing, others prefer dungeons, and a few crazy folks grind PvP for the challenge. Let's break down the fastest, most efficient paths to cap.

The Quest Route: Still the King for Most Players

Quest leveling in Midnight remains the most efficient and consistent method for average players. Why? Because the quest experience in this expansion is actually engaging -- the storylines are solid, and you're not feeling like you're grinding busywork.

Zone Progression: 80-85

Best Route
  • Isle of Dorn: Start here. Linear zone, excellent quest flow. You'll hit level 83 before you're done. Skip the optional side quests unless you need breaks.
  • Ohn'ahran Plains: Jump here at 83. This zone is spacious, so stick to the main quest hub. Mount up and don't waste time on treasures -- you're on a timer here.
  • Azure Span (partial): Pop over to Azure Span around level 85 if you want the fastest grind. Skip the breadcrumb quests at the start.

Zone Progression: 85-90

Endgame Push
  • Beledar's Totality (Nerub-ar Palace questing): This is the endgame zone, packed with high-XP quests. Some players report burning through levels 85-90 in 3-4 hours here.
  • Don't quest in both zones: Pick one and stick with it. The experience curves are similar, so just follow one storyline.

Quest Speed Tips

  • Use addons like BtWQuests to track quest progress without tab-clicking. Every second counts.
  • Turn off cut scenes and skip dialogue. You've heard the story before. Fast-forward everything.
  • Mount immediately when available. Pre-buy flying at level 81 if you haven't already.
  • Group up with others for elite quests. It'll actually speed things up.

The Dungeon Route: Faster Than You'd Think

Dungeons can actually be competitive with questing, especially if you're a healer or tank with instant queue times. As DPS, it's slower, but many players prefer the engagement over mindless questing.

Key Dungeons by Level

  • Levels 80-85: Brackenhide Hollow, Froststone Vault, and Scarlet Monastery keys (M0). Queue consistently.
  • Levels 85-88: Push low Mythic+ keys (M0-M2) as your level climbs. Higher difficulty = more XP.
  • Levels 88-90: Nerub-ar Palace dungeons become available. Run these on Mythic if you can.

Honestly? Dungeons are fun, but questing is 10-15% faster for most specs. Run dungeons if you want to gear up simultaneously or just need a break from questing.

The Hybrid Approach: Best for Efficiency

Here's what actually works best: Quest your way through zone chunks (2-3 levels), then run 5-10 dungeon runs to break the monotony.

This keeps your sanity intact while maintaining near-optimal leveling speed. You'll also pick up some blues and greens that smooth your stats for harder content at 90.

Consumables and Buffs That Actually Matter

  • Experience potions: Buy these from the Auction House (5% XP buff, 1 hour). They stack, so pop them before grinding sessions.
  • Well-Fed buff: Any food works. Get the XP version if it exists in your patch.
  • Guild perks: If your guild has them active, you're looking at a steady 5-10% XP boost.
  • Heirlooms: They're outdated for leveling speed now, but if you have them equipped, slight bonus.

Common Mistakes That Slow You Down

  • Teleporting everywhere. Run to quest hubs. It teaches you zone layout and saves time overall.
  • Looting everything. Turn off auto-loot or be selective. Vendor trash isn't worth the 3-second detour.
  • Doing world quests for XP. They're terrible XP-per-hour. Skip them entirely during the level push.
  • PvP at low levels. Unless you're genuinely skilled, PvP leveling will set you back hours.

What to Do at 90: Getting Raid-Ready

Once you hit 90, you're not done. You've got about 10 hours of gearing ahead before you're ready for organized raids or higher M+ keys. Start with:

  • Mythic 0 dungeons (quest version at 90)
  • PvP gear (if that's your jam)
  • Reputation gear from faction vendors
  • Crafted pieces from the auction house
