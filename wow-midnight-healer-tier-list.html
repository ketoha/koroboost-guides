WoW Midnight Healer Tier List

Season 1 healer rankings for M+ and Sunwell Reclaimed. Last updated March 2026.

← Back to All Guides

Healing in Midnight is rougher than anything we have seen since early Dragonflight. The Sunwell Reclaimed raid hits hard, the new Void Corruption debuff in M+ is a constant drain, and several dungeons have back-to-back damage spikes that will delete groups without a strong healer. After running hundreds of Sunwell Reclaimed carries, here is where every healer stands.

How We Rank Healers

We look at three things: raw healing throughput (can you keep the group alive during the worst moments), damage contribution (healers that deal more DPS help time keys and speed up boss kills), and utility (interrupts, dispels, CC, movement abilities). In Midnight, damage contribution matters more than ever because the +15 timer is extremely tight in at least three of the eight dungeons.

S-Tier

S Tier

Discipline Priest

Disc is the undisputed king of Midnight healing. Atonement healing received a rework that ties it to the new Silvermoon Radiance buff, giving Disc priests a passive 8% healing increase while in the Ghostlands zones (which includes four of eight M+ dungeons). Power Word: Shield absorbs are massive with the 4-piece tier bonus from Sunwell Reclaimed, averaging 420K per shield on a geared character. In raid, Disc handles Kael'thas Reborn's Pyroclasm phase better than any other healer thanks to preemptive Atonement spreading. Their damage output is also best in class for healers, regularly pulling 1.2M DPS in M+ packs.

Restoration Druid

Resto Druid got the rework it needed. The new Moonwell Bloom talent creates a ground effect that heals for 350K every 2 seconds to anyone standing in it, and it lasts 15 seconds on a 30-second cooldown. This is tailor-made for the stacking mechanics on Mythic Lor'themar and the Eredar Twins Reforged encounter. HoT-based healing also handles the Void Corruption affix perfectly since the damage ticks are predictable and steady. Their cat-weave damage in M+ is about 900K on trash, which is second only to Disc.

A-Tier

A Tier

Holy Paladin

HPal is the safest healer in Midnight. Lay on Hands, Blessing of Protection, and Blessing of Sacrifice are all clutch for Mythic progression. The problem is throughput. In heavy sustained damage phases like Dar'Khan Drathir's Shadow Barrage (12 seconds of raid-wide pulses every 1.5 seconds), HPal simply cannot keep up with Disc or Resto Druid's output. In M+, they are excellent up to +12, but in +15 and above, the damage intake outpaces their healing model. Still, their damage output (1.1M on trash) and utility keep them firmly in A-tier.

Preservation Evoker

Pres Evoker has the highest burst healing of any spec. Rewind is still the best "oh no" button in the game, and Dream Breath with the empowerment mechanic can top a full group from 20% to 100% in a single GCD. The downside is their 25-yard range, which is brutal in Sunwell Reclaimed where ranged groups are often spread 35+ yards apart. In M+, they are excellent because the group stays stacked. Expect them to rise in the meta as players learn to play around their range limitation.

Mistweaver Monk

Mistweaver is the dark horse of Midnight Season 1. The new Celestial Conduit talent procs off Renewing Mist, and with the tier set doubling Renewing Mist bounces, you get free burst healing windows every 20 seconds. Their mobility is unmatched (Roll, Transcendence, Tiger's Lust), making them the best healer for movement-heavy fights like Voren'thal the Seer and the Dawnstar Spire gauntlet. DPS contribution is average at 750K on trash.

B-Tier

B Tier

Restoration Shaman

Resto Shaman feels like it was designed for a different expansion. Spirit Link Totem is still incredible, but their throughput between cooldowns is the lowest of any healer. Healing Tide Totem was nerfed by 15% going into Midnight, and it shows. Chain Heal's bouncing mechanic does not work well with the spread positioning required on most Sunwell Reclaimed bosses. In M+, they are viable but you have to play perfectly. Interrupted Healing Surge casts will get someone killed. Their best asset is Wind Shear, the only ranged interrupt a healer has, which is valuable in Magisters' Terrace and Thalassian Depths where there are many priority caster mobs.

Holy Priest

Holy Priest has raw throughput in spades but no damage and minimal utility. Guardian Spirit is strong, and their sustained healing during long fights is actually competitive. The problem is M+. Holy Priest does 400K DPS on trash, which is nearly half of what Disc does. In a +15 where every second counts for the timer, that damage gap translates to 30-45 seconds over the course of a dungeon. In raid, Holy is actually close to A-tier, but we weight M+ equally in our rankings.

Healer Composition for Raid

For Mythic Sunwell Reclaimed, the optimal setup for a 20-player roster is: 1 Disc Priest, 1 Resto Druid, 1 Holy Paladin, and either a Pres Evoker or Mistweaver for the 4th spot. Disc handles the atonement blanket, Druid covers HoTs and Moonwell for stacking, HPal provides externals, and the 4th healer is your burst/flex role.

M+ Healer Advice

For keys below +10, play whatever you are comfortable with. The damage intake is manageable for all healers. For +10 to +14, you want Disc, Resto Druid, or HPal. For +15 and above, Disc Priest is strongly recommended unless you are an exceptional player on another spec. The Void Corruption affix at +12 and above adds constant ticking damage that favors proactive healers with strong passive throughput.
