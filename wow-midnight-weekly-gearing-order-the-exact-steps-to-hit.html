# WoW Midnight Weekly Gearing Order: The Exact Steps to Hit ilvl 285 So you hit 80 and now you're staring at a gear screen that looks like alphabet soup. Midnight's gearing is actually pretty straightforward if you know the order. Here's exactly what you should do each week to climb from 262 to 285 without wasting time on dead content. ## The First Week Priority When you hit 80, your first stop isn't the raid. It's delves. Yeah, really. Delves drop 262 gear for completing tier 8, which sounds bad until you realize you can spam them in like 20 minutes per character if you know the routes. This gets you to the minimum ilvl for pretty much everything else. A lot of players skip delves because they think it's "beneath them" or whatever. Wrong. Free gear is free gear. Hit those delves, grab your 262 pieces, then move to actual progression content. ## Weekly Quest Priority Here's the exact order for weekly quests: **First priority: Mythic+ Weekly** (ilvl 259-280 depending on key level). If you're a fresh 80, push keys at your own level first. Even +6 keys give solid loot. The bonus chest at the end is where the real value is. **Second: Raid Weekly** (ilvl 252-278). Normal Voidspire gives 278 gear. Heroic gives 291. If you can't raid mythic yet, this is your main progression tool. Do this weekly, no exceptions. **Third: PvP Weekly** (if you do PvP, 259-291). Honor gear starts at 259. Conquest gear is 285+. If you're not into rated PvP, skip this, but it's actually viable for fast gearing. ## The Daily Routine Every single day: **Hunting grounds** (spend your marks of the hunt immediately on gear upgrades, not cosmetics). These drops are 252-259 and they add up fast over a week. **World events** (if active). These rotate but always drop 252-259 gear. **Regular dungeons on higher difficulty**. Yeah, these are outdated for progression, but trash farming for shards is legit if you need specific pieces fast. ## Mid-Week Accelerator By Wednesday of each week, you should have run: - Your weekly Mythic+ key - Normal Voidspire raid (or skip if you're mythic-raiding) - Heroic Voidspire if your guild runs it This gets you to around 270-275 average ilvl. From there, it's just repeating the best sources. ## Crafted Gear (The Secret Tech) Midnight crafting got gutted compared to Nerub-ar, but it's still useful. Spark of Dreams + materials = 265 ilvl gear that costs like 5-10k gold depending on your server. Smart players are crafting their weakest slots while gearing other stuff. Belt, boots, bracers are usually cheap to craft. Do this while waiting for raid lockouts to reset. ## The Final Push to 285 Once you hit around 278 average ilvl: - **Run Mythic Voidspire** (291 drops). This is the only thing that matters now. - **Push higher Mythic+ keys** (285-305 gear). +12 keys and above start dropping 291. - **Heroic Voidspire** (285 gear) if you're not in a mythic-raiding guild. 285 is honestly not that hard to hit if you're doing at least 1-2 mythic raid nights per week. Most players hit it within 3-4 weeks of hitting 80. ## Delves As A Gold Maker Real talk: delves aren't just for fresh 80s. If you're geared, running delves for shards and flipping them is actually profitable. But that's a separate article about the economy. For now, just know that delves are always worth running, even once you're 285+. ## Don't Skip Heroic Voidspire A lot of try-hards skip heroic because they want mythic loot. But heroic's 285 gear is absolutely viable for pushing keys and getting into mythic-ready guilds. Plus, it's way more chill than mythic progression. If your guild isn't running heroic consistently, you're missing out on free upgrades. ## The Gearing Path for Alts If you're leveling alts, do the delves thing first (30 mins), then jump straight into normal raid. Most guilds will carry fresh 80s through normal for guild bonuses. This is way faster than trying to do keys at ilvl 262. ## Common Gearing Mistakes **Mistake 1**: Running low-level keys instead of pushing higher. Your time is worth more than farming +6 keys. Push your level, get better loot. **Mistake 2**: Ignoring crafting because you don't have mats. Buy the mats on the auction house. It's usually cheaper than running extra dungeons. **Mistake 3**: Waiting for "the perfect group" for mythic raid. Join a normal or heroic guild run. You'll learn more and gear faster with consistent groups. **Mistake 4**: Not doing weekly quests. These are literally free loot. Set a weekly reminder or you'll forget. ## Speed Up Your Gearing If you're in a hurry to hit 285, you can skip some steps. If you're hardcore about hitting max ilvl fast, that's always an option. ## The 285+ Grind Once you're 285, gearing slows way down. You're basically just running mythic raid weekly and pushing higher keys. The difference between 285 and 305 takes forever because mythic drops are rarer and you can only do the raid once a week. This is actually where the game gets fun though. You're no longer chasing ilvl; you're chasing better gear, better stats, perfect pieces. It's a totally different grind. ## Final Thoughts Midnight gearing is actually fast compared to past expansions. If you're even halfway serious, you'll hit 285 in like 3-4 weeks. Just stick to the order, do your weeklies, and don't get distracted by farming cosmetics or whatever. The sooner you're geared, the sooner you can actually start raiding with your guild and pushing real content. So yeah, buckle down, run your delves, and keep pushing. You got this.
