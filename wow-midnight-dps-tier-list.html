WoW Midnight DPS Tier List

Season 1 rankings for Mythic+ and Raids. Last updated March 2026.

← Back to All Guides

Three weeks into Midnight Season 1 and the meta is starting to settle. We have been running hundreds of Mythic+ carries and raid clears every day, and the data is clear: some specs are miles ahead of the pack. Here is where every DPS spec sits right now, with reasoning based on actual runs, not theorycrafting sims.

Methodology

Rankings are based on a combination of Warcraft Logs 95th percentile parses from Sunwell Reclaimed (Mythic), +15 key timing rates across all eight Midnight dungeons, and direct feedback from our carry team. We weight M+ and raid performance roughly 50/50 for the overall ranking, but we note where specs diverge.

S-Tier (Best in Slot)

S Tier

Frost Death Knight

Frost DK is absolutely dominating right now. The reworked Breath of Sindragosa synergizes with the new Void Rime conduit from the Ghostlands campaign to produce absurd burst windows. In M+, Frost DK brings consistent 4M+ overall DPS in +15s with uncapped AoE on pulls like the Magisters' Terrace trash corridor. In Sunwell Reclaimed, they are top 3 on every single boss except Lor'themar (movement-heavy). Their execute phase damage on Dar'Khan Drathir is unmatched.

Augmentation Evoker

Still mandatory. The Midnight-specific Arcane Threads talent node gives Augmentation another 4% party damage amp on top of everything they already had. Every serious group wants one. In raid, having an Aug on your melee camp during Kael'thas Reborn is the difference between a 6-minute kill and a wipe at 15%.

Subtlety Rogue

Sub got significant reworking going into Midnight and it paid off. The new Shadowstep mechanics let them teleport between Void Rifts in the Dawnstar Spire dungeon without losing uptime, and their single-target damage on Mythic Rommath is best-in-class. Shadow Dance windows with the Midnight 4-piece hit for 2.8M+ crits consistently.

A-Tier (Strong Picks)

A Tier

Fire Mage

Fire is back after a weak showing in The War Within Season 4. Combustion windows line up perfectly with the 1-minute pull cadence in dungeons like Thalassian Depths and Elrendar Crossing. Their damage profile is spiky but when it hits, nothing else comes close on 3-5 target packs. Raid performance is solid, hovering around 4th-5th on most encounters.

Unholy Death Knight

Unholy is the sustained AoE king. While Frost wins on burst, Unholy's Festering Wounds spread mechanic got a new talent that makes it spread to 8 targets baseline. In the Sunstrider Crypts dungeon where you pull 20+ mobs in the catacombs section, Unholy is doing 6-7M overall. Raid single target is slightly below average, which keeps it out of S-tier.

Devastation Evoker

Strong in both raid and M+. The new Eternity Surge empowerment from the Midnight talent tree lets Dev Evoker hit 5 targets with their main nuke. Excellent on Sunwell Reclaimed council fights. The only downside is their 25-yard range, which is punishing on Voren'thal the Seer's expanding void zones.

Retribution Paladin

Ret brings damage and utility. Their new Silvermoon Judgment talent adds a 10% damage taken debuff on bosses for 6 seconds, stacking with other debuffs. In M+, they are the best melee option if you are not running Sub Rogue. Wake of Ashes with the tier set is doing 1.5M per cast in AoE.

Affliction Warlock

Sleeper pick. Affliction's multidotting on the add-heavy fights in Sunwell Reclaimed (Eredar Twins Reforged, M'uru's Echo) puts them in the top 3 on those encounters. In M+, they are less dominant but still solid on Fortified weeks when trash lives long enough for dots to ramp.

B-Tier (Solid, Not Optimal)

B Tier

Fury Warrior - good damage but dies to everything. Needs a dedicated healer in high keys. Havoc Demon Hunter - nerfed too hard going into Midnight, their AoE cap at 8 targets hurts in the big-pull dungeons. Balance Druid - Starfall is still not competitive with other uncapped AoE. Enhancement Shaman - strong single target, weak AoE, awkward in M+. Windwalker Monk - Touch of Death cheese got fixed, now they are average everywhere. Demonology Warlock - tyrant windows are too long for M+ pull timing. Marksmanship Hunter - Aimed Shot build is top tier on pure single target bosses like Anveena's Guardian but falls off everywhere else.

C-Tier (Needs Buffs)

C Tier

Beast Mastery Hunter - pets die to every AoE mechanic in Sunwell Reclaimed. Frost Mage - ice lance build just cannot compete with fire's burst. Arms Warrior - bladestorm got nerfed, no reason to play this over Fury. Assassination Rogue - poison damage does not scale with the new Void Resistance affix. Destruction Warlock - Rain of Fire got an internal cooldown that killed their AoE. Outlaw Rogue - Roll the Bones variance makes them unreliable for timed content. Elemental Shaman - the rework missed the mark entirely.

What About Arcane Mage?

Arcane is in a weird spot. On paper, their numbers look B-tier. In practice, the Silvermoon Arcane Resonance buff you get inside Magisters' Terrace and Dawnstar Spire (two of the eight M+ dungeons) pushes them into A-tier for those specific dungeons. If you are only pushing keys in those two, Arcane is a dark horse. For everything else, play Fire.

Bottom Line

If you want to push +15s and above, Frost DK, Sub Rogue, and Aug Evoker are your safest bets. For raid, add Fire Mage and Affliction Warlock to that list. The meta will shift as Blizzard pushes tuning patches, but the current gap between S-tier and C-tier is significant. We are talking 25-30% DPS difference on most encounters.
