WoW Midnight PvP Gearing Guide

Conquest, Honor, and the fastest path to full PvP gear in Season 1.

Every season the same question floods reddit: "how do I gear for PvP?" And every season the answer changes because Blizzard reworks the system. Here's the no-BS path from fresh 90 to full PvP gear in Midnight.

Honor vs Conquest

CurrencySourceCapBuys
HonorRandom BGs, skirmishes, world PvP, PvP WQsNo capStarter PvP gear
ConquestRated arenas, rated BGs, weekly PvP quest~550/weekCompetitive PvP gear

The key insight: Honor gear is for getting started. Conquest gear is for competing. You need honor gear to survive long enough to earn conquest.

Week 1 The Honor Grind (Day 1-3)

Step 1: Buy PvP Trinkets First

Go to the PvP vendor. Buy both PvP trinkets with honor. The 2-piece set bonus gives CC break (mandatory) + stat proc. Without these, you get CC'd and die without counterplay. They are not optional.

Step 2: Fill Every Slot

Buy the cheapest honor gear for every slot. Stats don't matter yet, just fill slots. Cost: 8,000-12,000 honor. Takes 3-4 hours of random BGs.

Step 3: Queue for Everything

  • Random BGs: Best honor/hour (especially with daily first-win bonus)
  • Epic BGs (Ashran, AV): Good honor, less intense, easier if undergeared
  • Skirmishes: Fast queues, decent honor
  • PvP World Quests: Check daily, some give 300-500 honor in 2 minutes

Once you have a full honor set, you're ready for rated content.

Conquest Spending Priority

  1. Weapon first. Biggest damage upgrade. Always.
  2. Trinkets (if conquest trinkets are better than honor ones)
  3. Highest ilvl slots. Head, chest, legs give the most stats.
  4. Fill remaining. Shoulders, gloves, boots, belt, bracers, cloak, rings.

At 550 conquest/week, a full set takes ~4-5 weeks. Miss week 1 and you're permanently behind.

The Upgrade System

Rating directly improves your gear. Getting from 1000 to 1400 is a bigger gear upgrade than any single conquest purchase. Push rating, don't just farm conquest at 0.

PvE Gear in PvP: Does It Work?

Yes, but it's worse. Midnight has PvP stat scaling: PvP gear gets a bonus item level in instanced PvP. PvE gear doesn't.

  • Use PvE gear when: It's 20+ levels above your PvP piece, or you don't have a PvP piece for that slot yet
  • Never use PvE gear: Instead of PvP trinkets (CC break set bonus is mandatory), or when you have a conquest piece at any level

Enchants, Gems, and Stats

Why Versatility is King

In PvP, you take more damage than in PvE. Surviving one extra global cooldown often decides the fight. Versatility gives both offense AND defense. Stack it everywhere.

Consumables: Versatility phial (not DPS one), Versatility food, health potions (yes, use them -- they win duels), Healthstones from Warlock friends.

5 Common Gearing Mistakes

  1. Skipping honor gear and jumping into rated. You'll get destroyed and earn conquest slower. Spend 4-6 hours on honor gear first.
  2. Not capping conquest every week. Miss one week = 550 conquest behind forever. Cap every week. It takes 2-3 hours.
  3. Buying random conquest pieces. Follow the priority. Weapon first, not bracers because they're cheap.
  4. Ignoring rating breakpoints. Getting from 1000 to 1400 is a bigger upgrade than any single purchase. Push rating.
  5. Never enchanting PvP gear. Missing 3-4% versatility from gems/enchants is the difference between surviving a burst window and dying.

The Timeline

Fresh 90 to competitive PvP gear: about 3-4 weeks of moderate play (1-2 hours daily). Honor gear in 2 days, then push rating immediately. PvP gear stays relevant the entire season, unlike PvE which gets replaced every patch.
