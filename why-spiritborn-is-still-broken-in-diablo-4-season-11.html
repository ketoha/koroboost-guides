Why Spiritborn is Still Broken in Diablo 4 Season 11

A guide by Koroboost

Spiritborn dropped in Season 7 and the community collectively lost its mind. We're in Season 11 now, and yeah—they're STILL overtuned, but not in the way you think.

Here's the thing: Spiritborn isn't "overpowered" in the sense that they're busted solo. They're overpowered because they enable playstyles that literally don't work for other classes.

If you're not playing Spiritborn in Season 11 for Pit farming or Torment runs, you're handicapping yourself. Period.

What Makes Spiritborn Actually Broken

Most people point to Spiritborn's cooldown reduction or their synergies with spirit abilities. Sure, that's part of it. But the REAL reason they're broken is simpler:

Spiritborn has no playstyle weaknesses.

  • Need tankiness? Spirit of Stone (Druid) trivializes defense
  • Need damage? Spirit of Wrath (Demon Hunter) deletes everything
  • Need mobility? Spirit of Hurricane (Barbarian) + teleport = ungodly speed
  • Need sustain? Multiple spirits just heal you passively

Other classes have to pick a weakness. Spiritborn doesn't.

The Broken Spirit Synergies: How Spiritborn Actually Works

Spiritborn's broken power comes from chaining spirits together:

The Wrath Spirit Core Build:

  • Primary: Exploit claw attacks with Spirit of Wrath (Demon Hunter spirit)
  • Skill: Use Flurry or Shred (basic attacks that trigger claws)
  • Passive: Stack Ferocity (attack speed from spirits)
  • Result: You're dealing 800%+ damage per hit, and your cooldowns are gone instantly

This breaks EVERYTHING. Why? Because Exploit (from DH spirit) triggers on enemy weakness, and Spiritborn has multiple ways to apply weakness.

Damage numbers: You're doing 20-40 million damage per claw attack by Pit Tier 100.

For context? A Barbarian with a perfect build does 8-12 million. A Sorcerer does 10-15 million.

Spiritborn is doing double the damage while being TANKIER.

The Tankiness Creep Nobody Talks About

This is where Spiritborn gets truly ridiculous.

Spirit of Stone (Druid):

  • Creates a stone fortification that absorbs damage
  • Reapplies when you dodge
  • Costs nothing, triggers constantly
  • You literally can't die if you're dodging

A well-built Spiritborn with Stone has 150-200% Barrier (damage mitigation).

A Necromancer with a perfect build has 60-80% Barrier.

The difference between 150% and 80% is INSANE. That's an extra hit you survive that should have killed you.

Spirit of Hurricane (Barbarian):

  • Reduced cooldown duration by 25%
  • Movement speed boost
  • Knockback immunity

So you're mobile, you have cooldowns more often, and you can't get crowd controlled.

Why Other Classes Are Struggling

Let's be real: other classes are struggling BECAUSE Spiritborn is so overtuned.

Barbarians:

  • Need to manage fury (resource system)
  • Have to pick between offense and defense builds
  • Damage is good but not broken
  • Tankiness is solid but requires exact builds

Necromancers:

  • Minions die fast in high Tiers
  • Vulnerability application takes time
  • Need specific gear combinations
  • Can't AoE efficiently past Tier 80

Sorcerers:

  • Squishy without perfect defensive setup
  • Mana management is a real constraint
  • Cooldowns feel long between casts
  • Crowd control vulnerability

Rogues:

  • Need perfect gearing for shadows to work
  • Crowd control vulnerable
  • Cooldown dependent
  • Can't sustain in Tier 100 Pits

Spiritborn:

  • All of the above is OPTIONAL
  • Can scale tankiness AND damage simultaneously
  • Cooldown reduction means abilities are up constantly
  • Spirits cover every weakness

The Season 11 Meta Build: How to Abuse Spiritborn

If you want to see WHY Spiritborn is broken, build them this way:

Core Setup:

  • Spirit of Wrath (primary, use claw synergy)
  • Spirit of Stone (defensive layer)
  • Spirit of Hurricane (mobility + cooldown)
  • Spirit of Prey (Necromancer, bonus damage to vulnerable)

Skills:

  • Flurry (fast attack, triggers claws)
  • Shred (alternative fast attack)
  • Urban Decay (skill damage scaling, uses spirit mechanics)
  • Discipline (defensive ability)
  • Inner Sight (damage buff)

Passive Breakdown:

  • Ferocity (attack speed = more claws)
  • Toughness (defense)
  • Piercing Darkness (follow-up damage)
  • Puncturing Wounds (status effect damage)

Mechanics:

  • You press Flurry (costs nothing, scales with Ferocity)
  • Flurry triggers Spirit of Wrath's claw attacks
  • Claws apply Exploit
  • Exploit triggers from Prey vulnerability
  • You're doing 25+ million damage per attack
  • Meanwhile, Stone is absorbing incoming damage
  • Cooldowns are reduced by Hurricane

Result: You solo Pit Tier 100 in 12 minutes.

Other classes need 20+ minutes or specific grouping strategies.

Why This Isn't Getting Nerfed (And Why You Should Abuse It)

Blizzard's design philosophy in Season 11 is "let strong classes be strong." They're not aggressively nerfing anymore.

This means:

  • Spiritborn will stay overtuned all season
  • You SHOULD play Spiritborn if you want optimal farming
  • Other classes CAN compete, but it requires way more optimization
  • Seasonal conquest rewards go to Spiritborn players

If you're trying to farm Pit efficiently or push Torment tiers, not playing Spiritborn is leaving free loot on the table.

The Spiritborn Experience: What Changes at Different Tiers

Tiers 1-50 (Campaign + early endgame):

  • Spiritborn is strong but not yet broken
  • Any competent build works
  • Just level your character normally

Tiers 50-80 (Pit farming sweet spot):

  • Spiritborn starts dominating
  • Other classes can compete but need specific builds
  • Spiritborn can use basically any skill and still farm fast
  • Farm time: 10-15 minutes per Pit run

Tiers 80-100 (Late endgame grind):

  • Spiritborn is absolutely unmatched
  • A GOOD Spiritborn build deletes entire rooms
  • A BAD Spiritborn build still survives easily
  • Farm time: 12-18 minutes per Pit run
  • Other classes: 20-30+ minutes

Torment 1-5 (World Tier):

  • Spiritborn can push higher World Tiers faster
  • Can farm Torment efficiently solo
  • Other classes require grouping or specific builds

The Real Problem: Scaling Doesn't Have Limits

The actual issue with Spiritborn is that their power scaling doesn't hit a wall like other classes.

Other classes: At Tier 100, you hit a damage ceiling. You've stacked multipliers, you're doing your max damage.

Spiritborn: At Tier 100, you STILL have items slots open, you STILL have passive points, and you STILL have spirit combination possibilities that increase your power.

They scale past where the content gets hard.

How to Counter-Play (If You're Not Playing Spiritborn)

If you're committed to a different class, here's how to NOT get left behind:

Barbarians:

  • Spam Upheaval (AoE destruction)
  • Stack Bleed + Vulnerable application
  • Use War Cry for cooldown reduction (mimics Spiritborn's advantage)
  • Push crowd control immunity

Necromancers:

  • Use Bone Prison for consistent Vulnerability
  • Stack minion damage stats aggressively
  • Use Corpse Explosion for burst
  • Group farming is superior to solo farming

Sorcerers:

  • Use Frozen Orb for consistent hits
  • Stack Vulnerable application (Chill synergy)
  • Don't try to match Spiritborn's tankiness, embrace glass cannon
  • Rely on positioning and crowd control

Rogues:

  • Spec into Shadow damage for consistency
  • Stack Poison + Vulnerability
  • Use Shadow clones for sustained pressure
  • Accept that solo Pit is slower than Spiritborn

The Controversial Take

Spiritborn will probably get nerfed in Season 12. Blizzard doesn't like having one class this dominant for two full seasons. But right now? Abuse them.

If you're on the fence about whether to reroll Spiritborn, the answer is YES. You're leaving weeks of farm efficiency on the table by sticking with another class.

The meta will shift eventually. But for the next 3 months? Spiritborn is the optimal choice by a massive margin.

Bottom Line

Spiritborn is broken because Blizzard created a class that does everything: damage, tankiness, cooldown reduction, and utility. No other class can claim all four simultaneously.

Play them while they're overtuned. Optimize your build. Farm Pit efficiently. Reap the seasonal rewards.

Other classes will get buffed eventually. But until then, the answer to "which class should I farm with?" is Spiritborn—not because it's the only way to play, but because it's the most efficient way.
