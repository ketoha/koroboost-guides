M+ Healer Tier List & Survival Guide

Midnight Season 1 healer rankings for Mythic+ dungeons. Updated March 2026.

← Back to All Guides

Healer queues in Mythic+ dungeons are almost non-existent right now -- which is great for getting fast invites, but it also means you're under pressure to not wipe your group. Whether you're pushing high keys or just farming +8s for vault gear, your healer choice matters more than ever in Midnight Season 1.

S-Tier: The Absolute Safest Picks

S-Tier: Holy Priest

Still the king. Holy Priest has everything: proactive healing with Power Word: Shield, reactionary healing with Heal/Flash Heal, and enough defensive utility to carry a group that doesn't know mechanics.

  • Strengths: Instant shields, strong single-target healing, Divine Aegis shields stack, Pain Suppression is clutch on tank.
  • Weaknesses: Mana management requires attention. Not great at recovering groups from wipe-mechanics.
  • Damage: Decent. You can do meaningful damage between heals.

Verdict: Pick this if you want zero surprises and maximum forgiving mechanics.

S-Tier: Restoration Druid

Resto druids have exploded in Midnight. The new talent tree finally gives them the tools to compete in dungeons, not just raids.

  • Strengths: Wild Growth for AOE, Rejuvenation covers your whole group, insane mobility with Travel Form, Ironbark is a solid defensive.
  • Weaknesses: Weak on tank healing until you're geared. Overgrowth takes planning.
  • Damage: Can pump between heals with Moonfire spam.

Verdict: Great pick if your group isn't a complete mess. High skill ceiling.

A-Tier: Solid and Viable

A-Tier: Holy Paladin
  • Strengths: Flash of Light is instant and punchy, Divine Purpose procs are free heals, Lay on Hands is a panic button, Blessing of Protection saves dumb DPS from one-shots.
  • Weaknesses: Mana-intensive. No AOE healing comparable to other specs. Single-target focused.
  • Damage: Basically zero. You're healing-only.

Verdict: Pick if you have a bursty group and want to save individual players.

A-Tier: Restoration Shaman
  • Strengths: Chain Heal for group coverage, Healing Stream Totem is passive regen, Spirit Link Totem trivializes massive AOE damage.
  • Weaknesses: Totem management is a chore. Healing is slow without procs.
  • Damage: Can Lava Burst between heals.

Verdict: Solid if you're experienced. Punishing if you're not paying attention to totems.

B-Tier: Playable But Challenging

B-Tier: Mistweaver Monk
  • Strengths: Insane mobility, Vivify spreads heals, Enveloping Mist is strong single-target, chi-generating offence.
  • Weaknesses: Horrible mana management. Takes skill to shine. Vulnerable if caught in mechanics.

Verdict: Only pick if you're experienced. Casual monks will struggle in high keys.

B-Tier: Discipline Priest
  • Strengths: Insane damage output, Atonement healing is elegant, Schism adds flavor.
  • Weaknesses: Your healing is tied to damage. If you don't have uptime, people die.
  • Damage: Top-tier. You're competing with DPS.

Verdict: Play this if your group is geared and doesn't need hand-holding.

Survival Tips That Actually Work

Know Your Tank's Cooldowns

Your tank has damage cooldowns that are also defensive cooldowns. Know when they're active. Don't overextend healing -- let their defensives do the work.

Learn the Dungeon Walls

Dungeons have burst damage peaks -- moments where the group takes massive damage. Learn when they happen in each dungeon. Prepare your big heals for those moments, not for chip damage.

Mana Matters More Than Health Bars

A healer with 100% health and 30% mana is close to a wipe. Monitor your mana first. Your group's health bar is secondary.

Position Yourself Correctly

Stay back from the group enough that you're not in cleave range, but close enough that you can move quickly to save dumb DPS.

Gearing Your Healer Fast

  • Start with Mythic 0 dungeon gear (quest at 90).
  • Farm +5 to +8 for vault gear and more blues.
  • Prioritize Intellect + Haste. Crit and Vers are secondary.
  • Buy crafted gear from the AH if you're impatient.
