Why Most Players Get Stuck

Most players hit a wall around Pit 100-110 and assume they need better gear. That is part of it, but execution matters just as much. Here are the real reasons people fail and how to fix them.

Tip 1: Elixirs Are Not Optional

Every Pit has a dominant damage type. Match your elixir to it. Running a fire resistance elixir in a poison-heavy Pit means you are leaving 20-30% survivability on the table. Check the affixes before entering and swap accordingly.

Best elixirs for pushing: Iron Barb Elixir (armor), Assault Elixir (attack speed), and the correct resistance elixir for the Pit type.

Tip 2: Skip White Mobs

White mobs give almost zero progress. Elite packs give 10-15x more. At high Pits, every second matters. Run past trash, find elites, kill them, move on. A good Pit run focuses 80% of time on elite packs.

Tip 3: Reroll Bad Maps

Not all Pit layouts are equal. Cathedral maps are dense and fast. Open field maps are sparse and slow. If you get a bad layout, leave and re-enter. You lose 30 seconds but save 2-3 minutes on clear time.

Best layouts: Cathedral, Sewers, Halls. Worst: Open Desert, Forest Paths.

Tip 4: Tempering Beats Item Level

One perfect temper roll on your weapon is worth more than 5 item level upgrades across your whole gear. Before spending materials on Masterworking, make sure your tempers are right. Reroll bad tempers first.

Priority temper slots: weapon (always first), amulet, gloves. These three have the highest damage scaling in the game.

Tip 5: Learn Boss Patterns

Most deaths in high Pits happen at the boss. You clear the whole Pit cleanly, then die 3 times on the boss and fail the timer. Every boss has a tell before their big attack. Learn the wind-up animation and dodge BEFORE the hit, not after.

Hardest bosses: Herald of Malphas (3 slam combo), Butcher spawns (instant charge), Uber Lilith variants (ground corruption).

Bonus: The 2-Minute Rule

If you are not at 50% progress by the 2-minute mark, you will not clear the Pit in time. Leave and try again. Do not waste 5 minutes on a doomed run. Efficient pushing means knowing when to reset.

What Pit Level Should You Target?

  • Pit 80-100: Item level 900+, basic tempers done
  • Pit 100-120: Item level 925+, all tempers optimized, 2+ Masterwork upgrades
  • Pit 120-140: Near-perfect gear, 4+ Masterwork on key pieces, boss patterns memorized
  • Pit 140-150: God-tier gear, flawless execution, S-tier build required
