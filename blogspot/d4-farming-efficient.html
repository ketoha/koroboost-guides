Diablo 4: How to Farm Gold and Materials Efficiently in Season 11

If you're running Diablo 4 endgame and NOT making 50 million gold per hour, you're wasting your time. Seriously. Bad farming routes will have you making 15-20 million. Good farming will have you at 70+ million.

The difference is entirely about ROUTE OPTIMIZATION and understanding which content gives the best gold-to-time ratio.

I've spent this whole season testing different farming patterns, and I've found three strategies that are unmatched. If you use even ONE of these, your gold income will double.

Strategy 1: Pit Farming - The Consistent Gold Grind

The premise: Run Pit Tiers 80-90 repeatedly. Quick clears, solid loot, predictable gold.

Why it works:

  • Pit runs take 12-18 minutes depending on your class
  • Gold per run: 3-5 million (before item selling)
  • Item selling value: 8-15 million (rare/unique drops)
  • Total per run: 11-20 million

    • The math: At 15-minute Pit runs with 15 million gold per run, you're making:

  • 60 million per hour (4 runs)

    • But wait—that's JUST from clearing. Add in item selling and salvage materials, and you're at 80-100 million per hour if you're optimal.

    The key to Pit farming efficiency:

    1. Route optimization: Know the spawn patterns. Some bosses spawn in predictable locations. Skip unnecessary areas.

    2. Speed build vs. tankiness: You want speed with ENOUGH tankiness. Not full glass cannon, not full tank.

    3. Item sorting: Don't stop to examine every drop. Pick up rares/uniques, salvage everything else AFTER the run.

    4. Sell fast: Go directly to vendor after 3-4 runs, sell stacks of items for time-saving.

    Gold per hour at Tier 90 (good gear): 70-90 million

    Strategy 2: Torment Farming - The High-Risk/High-Reward Route

    The premise: Run World Tier 4 (Torment) in targeted zones, farms bosses on repeat.

    Why it works:

  • Torment content drops EXCLUSIVELY high-tier loot
  • Unique items are 5x more likely than lower Tiers
  • Boss fights are repeatable (kill boss, leave, restart area, repeat)
  • Item value is MUCH higher

    • The catch: Torment is actually dangerous. You need specific gear and builds. One mistake = death = wasted 15 minutes.

    The actual flow:

    1. Pick a Torment zone with fast boss access (Scosglen has several fast bosses)

    2. Clear to boss (5 minutes)

    3. Kill boss (2-3 minutes)

    4. Leave, restart area, repeat

    5. Every 2-3 runs, sell your items

    Gold per hour at Torment (excellent gear): 100-150 million

    Here's the thing though: This requires near-perfect gear. If you're not at gear cap with optimized affixes, Torment farming is SLOW and dangerous.

    Reality check: Most players shouldn't attempt this. Pit Tier 90 is the safer path.

    Strategy 3: Helltide Farming - The Underrated Option

    This is the one people overlook, but it's ABSURDLY efficient if you know what you're doing.

    The premise: Farm Helltide events, specifically targeting high-spawning elite packs.

    Why it works:

  • Helltide mobs drop TONS of loot quickly
  • Guaranteed high-value item drops (guaranteed uniques roughly every 10-15 minutes of active farming)
  • No boss fights = consistent clear speed
  • Can be done on lower Tiers = safer for mid-geared characters

    • The actual flow:

    1. Wait for Helltide event (happens every 2 hours)

    2. Load into highest accessible Tier

    3. Target densely populated areas (Dry Steppes, Kehjistan)

    4. Farm elite packs for 20 minutes

    5. Open Helltide chest (guaranteed unique + rare drops)

    6. Restart area, repeat

    Gold per hour: 60-80 million (less than Pit, but way safer)

    Why this is underrated: Most players don't farm Helltides because they think Pits are "better." But here's the thing—if you're averaging 15-minute Pit clears, you're only getting 4 clears per hour. Helltides let you farm 3-4 complete cycles per 2-hour window with LESS risk.

    The Math Behind Gold Efficiency

    Let me break down the actual gold formula:

    Gold per hour = (Gold per run + (Item value / 1,000,000)) / Time per run in minutes × 60

    Pit Tier 90 Example:

  • Gold per run: 3.5 million
  • Average item value: 12 million
  • Time per run: 15 minutes
  • Formula: (3.5 + 12) / 15 × 60 = 62 million per hour

    • Helltide Example:

  • Gold per event (20-minute window): 8 million
  • Average unique/rare value: 15 million per event
  • Time: 20 minutes (1/6 of Helltide cycle)
  • Formula: (8 + 15) / 20 × 60 = 69 million per hour

    • Torment Farming Example:

  • Gold per run: 5 million
  • Average item value: 25 million
  • Time per run: 12 minutes
  • Formula: (5 + 25) / 12 × 60 = 150 million per hour

    • The numbers prove it: Torment > Helltide ≥ Pit (in terms of raw gold)

    BUT: Torment requires perfect gear. If you can't consistently clear fast, Pit farming wins.

    Materials: Where to Farm Specific Drops

    Gold is one thing. But you also need crafting materials.

    Copper/Silver/Gold: Farm any endgame content (Pits, Helltides, Torment)

    Scarlet Shards (Rares): Drop from rare enemies. Pit Tier 80+ gives consistent drops.

    Veiled Crystals (Uniques): Only drop from Unique/Boss enemies. Farm Pits Tier 90+ or Torment for consistent supply.

    Forgotten Souls (Upgrade material): Drop from ANY endgame enemy at low rate. Farm whatever gives best gold, soul drops happen naturally.

    Gem Fragments: Only farm dungeons if you specifically need gems. Otherwise, don't—it's inefficient.

    The Underrated Gold Hack: Item Flipping

    This is where people make REAL money, but it requires market knowledge.

    How it works:

    1. Farm Pits and identify dropped items

    2. Look for underpriced unique items in player markets

    3. Buy them if the price is 20%+ below average

    4. Sell 24 hours later when market resets

    5. Profit: 5-10 million per flip (if you find good deals)

    Reality: This takes market watching skill. But if you're buying/selling 3-4 items per farming session, you're adding 15-40 million pure profit.

    This is why experienced farmers make 100+ million per hour—they're not just farming, they're trading.

    Farming Optimization: The Real Gold Multipliers

    You want to jump from 50 million/hour to 100 million/hour? Focus on these:

    1. Kill Speed:

  • Faster kills = faster runs
  • Every 1-minute reduction in clear time = 4 million extra per hour
  • Optimize your build for AoE + single-target balance

    • 2. Movement Speed:

  • Faster run throughput
  • Every 10% movement speed = roughly 3% faster runs
  • Stack it aggressively

    • 3. Magic Find:

  • More rares/uniques drop
  • Each 5% Magic Find roughly = 1-2 million extra per hour in item value
  • Don't sacrifice damage for it (bad trade-off)

    • 4. Loot Organization:

  • Picking up garbage slows you down
  • Develop a "loot hierarchy": pick up uniques/rares, skip commons
  • Salvage after runs, not during

    • 5. Energy Management:

  • Don't waste abilities on overkill
  • Single enemy? Don't use your AoE ability
  • Boss? Burn cooldowns without hesitation
  • Pacing = efficiency

    Seasonal Economy Reality

    Here's something important: Farming gold becomes LESS valuable as season progresses.

  • Week 1-2: Gold is extremely valuable (buying gear)
  • Week 3-4: Gold is moderately valuable (upgrades)
  • Week 5+: Gold is cheap (most players are geared)

    • If you want to optimize gold farming, do it EARLY season. Later season, focus on specific item farming instead (Uniques, build enabling drops).
