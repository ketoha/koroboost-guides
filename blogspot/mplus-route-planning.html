Mythic+ Route Planning: Stop Wasting Time on Bad Pulls

I've watched literally hundreds of M+ runs explode because of bad pulling strategy. Not because the group wasn't geared—because they pulled 7 trash packs instead of 4, added 8 minutes to the clear, and wiped at the boss from a timer mechanic.

Good routing cuts your clear time by 15-25% WITHOUT adding difficulty. Bad routing turns a 30-minute key into a 45-minute fail.

The crazy part? Route optimization is completely free. You don't need better gear. You need better THINKING.

Why Routing Matters in M+: The Math

Scenario 1: Bad Route

  • Pull 15 trash groups before boss
  • Clear time: 38 minutes
  • Boss wipe: 5 minutes
  • Total time: 43 minutes
  • Result: Timer expired, no loot

    • Scenario 2: Optimized Route

  • Pull 9 trash groups, skip 6 packs entirely
  • Clear time: 29 minutes
  • Clean boss kill: 3 minutes
  • Total time: 32 minutes
  • Result: Timer with 8 minutes cushion

    • What changed? Nothing except the route. Same gear, same group, same dungeon.

    The difference is 11 minutes and a failed key vs. a timed run.

    The Three Questions That Define Your Route

    Before you pull a single mob, ask:

    1. What's the minimum trash damage needed to progress?

    Each M+ dungeon has a "Trash Damage Done" requirement. You MUST kill enough trash to unlock the next area. But there's a catch—you only need to kill ENOUGH, not every single pack.

    Example: Shadowmoon Burial Grounds M+18 has a 98% trash damage requirement.

  • Total trash available: 145%
  • Requirement: 98%
  • Acceptable kills: Any combination equaling 98%+

    • This means you have CHOICES. You can skip 47% of trash entirely and still open the boss.

    2. Which packs give the most efficient damage-to-pull ratio?

    Some trash packs die in 20 seconds. Some take 3 minutes.

  • 5-mob pack in 20 seconds: 25 damage per second
  • 8-mob pack in 3 minutes: 2.7 damage per second

    • The 5-mob pack is 9x MORE efficient. Priority should be those.

    3. What mechanics are forcing me to adjust the route?

    Some dungeons have unavoidable trash (corridor trash you literally have to pass through). Some have optional side areas.

    Efficient routing respects both types.

    The Core Routing Principle: Damage Per Minute Optimization

    Here's the framework that changes everything:

    Damage/Minute = Trash% killed / Time spent (minutes)

    Example Route Comparison (Spires of Ascension M+18):

    Route A (Standard):

  • Pull every trash pack you see
  • Damage: 100%
  • Time: 35 minutes
  • DPM: 2.86

    • Route B (Optimized):

  • Pull only required trash + high-efficiency packs
  • Damage: 98%
  • Time: 28 minutes
  • DPM: 3.5

    • Route B is 22% faster and technically better.

    This is your routing mantra: maximize DPM, not DPS.

    How to Know What's Mandatory Trash

    This is the skill most M+ players lack.

    Mandatory trash = blocking your path to the next section

    Optional trash = in side rooms, off the main path, or before gates

    How to identify mandatory:

  • Is there a gate/door blocking you? You need trash damage to open it.
  • Is the path clear? You don't HAVE to pull anything.
  • Is there a hallway with mobs? That's mandatory.

    • Test: If you can skip a pack and still be able to walk forward, it's optional.

    Routing for Different Team Compositions

    Your route changes based on what your group can handle:

    High-Damage Group (Rogue/DH/Warlock DPS focus):

  • Pull MORE packs simultaneously (you have burst)
  • Route should include trash density (big packs, multiple packs)
  • Time is less important than kill speed
  • Route: Aggressive 5+ mobs at a time

    • Balanced Group (Mixed DPS + good healer):

  • Pull medium packs (3-4 mobs)
  • Optimize for both damage and safety
  • Standard route works fine
  • Route: Controlled pulls, balanced pace

    • Defensively Built Group (Tank-heavy, limited DPS):

  • Pull FEWER mobs per pack
  • Route should minimize overpull potential
  • Emphasize skippable trash
  • Route: Conservative single-pack pulls, skip risky options

    • The Healer's Routing Perspective

    Healers experience routing VERY differently than DPS.

    What makes routing hard for healers:

  • Overpull (too many mobs = you can't heal everyone)
  • Multi-pack pulls (raid-wide damage spikes)
  • Slow kill times (mana drain)

    • Healer-friendly routes:

  • Pull fewer mobs at once
  • Space out difficult packs (let healer Mana regenerate)
  • Avoid situations where 2+ hard packs aggro together
  • Route: Conservative, predictable, healer-centric

    • If your healer is struggling, your route is too aggressive.

    The Mistake That Costs Keys: Overpull Cascades

    This is how good runs become failures:

    1. Pull pack A (intended)

    2. Someone walks into pack B (accidental aggro)

    3. Tank panics, doesn't kite

    4. Healer runs out of Mana

    5. Someone dies

    6. Entire group dies

    7. Run fails

    How to prevent this:

  • Know the geometry of each room
  • Pull with purpose, not randomly
  • Use LINE OF SIGHT to separate packs
  • Communicate before pulls ("About to pull left side, healer prepare")

    • Routing Strategy: The Path Hack

    Here's a mental framework I use:

    Step 1: Identify all possible trash

    Look at the dungeon layout, note every mob pack.

    Step 2: Rank by Efficiency

  • Elite packs: High priority (kill fast, give big progression)
  • Small groups: Low priority (skip if possible)
  • Mandatory corridors: Required (kill regardless)

    • Step 3: Calculate Minimum Path

    What's the LEAST amount of killing required to hit 98% damage + reach boss?

    Step 4: Group Pulls

    Can you pull 2 packs at once safely? Do it (save time). Dangerous together? Don't.

    Step 5: Practice the Route Twice

    Run it the same way next time so everyone memorizes it.

    Common Routing Mistakes to Stop Making

    Mistake 1: Pulling "Everything" for faster timer

  • This doesn't work
  • You'll fail timer AND die
  • Route conservatively first, push only if you have surplus time

    • Mistake 2: Not communicating the route

  • New group? Explain route in voice chat
  • Point out what you're skipping and why
  • Takes 30 seconds, prevents 5 minutes of wasted pulls

    • Mistake 3: Allowing random aggressive pulls

  • If DPS is pulling ahead of tank, that's a run-killer
  • Establish: "Only the tank pulls"
  • DPS follows, doesn't lead

    • Mistake 4: Bad positioning on packs

  • Always pull trash AWAY from next trash pack
  • Use pillars/geometry to block line of sight
  • Don't accidentally aggro 2 packs with bad spacing

    • Mistake 5: Ignoring healer mana

  • Healer at 30% mana? Don't pull another pack
  • Let them regenerate (takes 8-10 seconds)
  • Better to wait 10 seconds than wipe in 2 minutes

    The Reality Check: When to Push vs. Preserve

    Sometimes you're running ahead of timer. Sometimes you're behind.

    Ahead of timer (>5 minutes surplus)?

  • Pull more aggressively
  • Skip fewer packs
  • Optimize for speed

    • On timer (3-5 minute buffer)?

  • Stick to optimized route
  • Don't deviate
  • Play it safe

    • Behind timer (<3 minutes)?

  • You already lost
  • Finish cleanly for practice
  • Don't wipe trying to catch up

    • Pushing an already-lost key just teaches bad habits. Play it clean, learn the route, come back stronger next time.

    How Professional M+ Teams Route

    Serious M+ teams have their routes MEMORIZED.

    They discuss:

    1. Exact pull order

    2. LOS positioning for each pack

    3. Who handles adds/elites

    4. Cooldown assignments per pack

    5. Timeline targets ("Should be at boss by 18:00 mark")

    This level of routing lets them hit 2+ minute timers on keys that should take 30 minutes.

    You don't need to be that hardcore, but the principle applies: Preparation beats improvisation.
