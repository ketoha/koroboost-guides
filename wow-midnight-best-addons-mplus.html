15 Best WoW Addons for Mythic+

The only addon list you need for Midnight Season 1. No filler, no garbage. Tested across hundreds of keys.

← Back to All Guides

There are thousands of WoW addons. Most of them are garbage for M+ players. I've been pushing keys since Legion and I've tried probably 200 addons over the years. Deleted most of them. The ones that survived are the ones I literally cannot play without.

This isn't a list of "every addon that exists." This is the list of addons that will measurably improve your M+ performance in Midnight Season 1.

Tier 1 Absolutely Mandatory

1 WeakAuras

If you only install one addon, make it this one. WeakAuras lets you create custom visual alerts for literally anything in the game. Boss casting a frontal? Giant red icon on your screen. Your interrupt is off cooldown? Green border glow. Your buff is about to expire? Countdown timer.

The real power: the community. Go to wago.io and search for your class + "M+" and you'll find hundreds of pre-built packs.

Must-have packs for Midnight:

  • Your class rotation pack (search "[class] Midnight Season 1" on wago.io)
  • Midnight dungeon pack (boss abilities, interrupt alerts, dodge warnings)
  • Party cooldown tracker (see when your group has defensives/interrupts ready)

2 Details! (Damage Meter)

You need to know your numbers. Not for ego, but for diagnosing problems. If you're timing keys easily, Details tells you why. If you're depleting, it tells you why too.

Pro tip: Open Details after a wipe, click the Deaths tab, and click on whoever died first. It shows you the exact sequence of damage events that killed them. This is how you fix wipe patterns.

3 BigWigs / Deadly Boss Mods (DBM)

Pick one. Not both. These addons show boss timers, mechanic warnings, and ability alerts. BigWigs is lighter and more customizable. DBM is louder and more aggressive with warnings.

For M+ specifically, they alert you to boss ability timers, trash pack casts, enrage timers, and phase transitions.

4 Plater Nameplates

Default nameplates are useless. Plater replaces them with customizable health bars above every mob.

  • Color-code mobs by type (casters = red, melees = blue, important = gold)
  • Show cast bars on nameplates (see what every mob is casting without targeting them)
  • Show interrupt cooldowns on nameplates
  • Show threat status (losing aggro? nameplate turns red)

Plater is the difference between "what killed me?" and seeing the problem 3 seconds before it happens.

Tier 2 Strongly Recommended

5 OmniCD

Shows your party's cooldowns in a compact display. You can see exactly when your healer has their big cooldown ready, when DPS have defensives, and when interrupts are available. Instead of asking "do you have kick?" you just look and know.

6 Method Dungeon Tools (MDT)

The route planning addon. Shows dungeon maps with every trash pack, their trash count percentages, and lets you plan pull-by-pull routes. If you're the tank or the leader, this is non-negotiable. Import routes from top M+ players, customize them, and share with your group.

7 GTFO

Plays an alarm sound when you're standing in bad stuff. That's it. That's the addon. Sounds simple. Saved my life thousands of times. When you're focused on your rotation and don't notice the pool of fire under your feet, GTFO screams at you.

8 Hekili (for DPS)

A rotation helper that shows you what button to press next. Even top players use it as a backup. In the chaos of M+ trash pulls with 8 mobs and 4 mechanics happening, having a rotation suggestion in the corner keeps your DPS consistent. It doesn't play for you. It suggests the optimal next ability based on your current buffs, debuffs, and targets.

9 ElvUI or TukUI

A complete UI replacement. The default WoW UI wastes screen space. ElvUI compacts everything into a clean, minimal layout that gives you more screen visibility for mechanics. Setup takes 20 minutes. After that, you'll never go back.

Tier 3 Nice to Have

10 Angry Keystones

Shows the M+ timer, pull count percentage, and death counter in a clean format. Also shows weekly affixes and their effects.

11 Auto Repair & Sell Junk

Two tiny addons that auto-repair your gear at vendors and auto-sell gray items. Save 30 seconds after every dungeon run.

12 Mythic Dungeon Tools

Shows trash count percentage in real-time as you kill mobs. Helps you know exactly how close you are to the threshold for boss progression.

13 SimulationCraft

The addon exports your character data. Paste it into raidbots.com and it tells you optimal stat priorities, best gear combinations, and expected DPS. Do this every time you get new gear.

14 Raider.IO

Shows M+ scores on character tooltips and in the group finder. Helps you assess whether a group is likely to time the key. Also tracks your own score progression.

15 LittleWigs

Extension for BigWigs that adds M+ trash alerts. The base BigWigs only covers bosses. LittleWigs adds warnings for dangerous trash abilities, which is arguably MORE important in M+ than boss mechanics.

30-Minute Setup Guide

Step 1: Install CurseForge app (curseforge.com). This manages all your addons.

Step 2: Search and install all 15 addons from the list above.

Step 3: Launch WoW. Priority setup order:

  1. ElvUI: Run the setup wizard, pick DPS/Healer/Tank profile
  2. WeakAuras: Go to wago.io, find your class pack, import it
  3. Plater: Import a popular profile from wago.io (search "Plater M+ profile")
  4. BigWigs/DBM: Enable it, default settings are fine
  5. Everything else: Default settings work out of the box

Step 4: Run a low key (+2 or +5) to test. Adjust positions of UI elements between pulls.

The Addon Trap

Don't install 50 addons. More addons means more memory usage, more FPS drops, and more visual clutter. The goal is information clarity, not information overload. 15 addons is enough. Anything beyond that is diminishing returns.

Having the right addons is table stakes. They give you the information, but you have to act on it. The best addon in the world won't help if you don't actually interrupt the cast it's warning you about.

If you want to see how high-key players actually use these tools in practice, running alongside experienced M+ teams lets you observe their WeakAura setups, pull strategies, and cooldown management in real time. It's one thing to have the addons. It's another to see them used at a high level.
