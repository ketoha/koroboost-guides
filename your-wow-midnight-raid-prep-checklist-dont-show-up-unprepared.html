Your WoW Midnight Raid Prep Checklist: Don't Show Up Unprepared

A guide by Koroboost

Look, I've been raiding since Vanilla, and the number of people who show up to raid nights without basic prep is absolutely wild. We're talking no flasks, missing pots, consumables at zero, and specs that haven't been gemed out since Tuesday reset. It's 2026, folks. The raid meta isn't forgiving anymore, and Blizzard designed Midnight bosses with actual DPS checks in mind.

If you're serious about clearing Midnight raid content without holding your guild back, here's the exact checklist I use before every single lockout. This has saved me countless wipes and probably saved my guild leader from benching me at least once.

Consumables: The Non-Negotiable Tier

This is where 90% of raiders fail. You need flask + food + potions, period. No excuses.

Flasks:

  • Grab a Phantom Power Flask if you're DPS (primary stat boost for 1 hour)
  • Flask of Fortified Power is your fallback if you run out
  • Healers need Intellect Flask for that raw healing throughput
  • Cost: roughly 80-120g per flask depending on your server
  • Buy them on Tuesday after reset, not 10 minutes before raid

Food Buffs:

  • Main stats: Sated Feasts (the feasted version gives +35 primary stat, huge for DPS)
  • Healers: Intellect-focused feasts
  • Pro tip: if your guild doesn't provide feasts, bring Individual Feast potions (they stack with the feast stat buff if someone's already got a guild feast rolling)
  • Have AT LEAST 10 of these in your bags

Potions:

This is where people cheap out, and then wonder why they're not parsing well.

For DPS: Potent Power Potions (pre-pull, 30% primary stat for 25 seconds)

  • Use one before each boss pull
  • Use a second potion at ~60% boss health for the final burn phase
  • Buy 20 per raid night minimum

For Healers: Potion of Intellect and Potion of Healing (not the trash ones, the good ones)

  • Keep 10 of each

For Tanks: Fortification Potions (stamina boost, helps tank big mechanics)

  • 5-8 per raid

Enchants, Gems, Flasks: The Permanent Stuff

Before raid night, I'm talking the day before or even earlier in the week:

Gems:

Every. Single. Socket. Needs a gem.

  • DPS: Primary stat gems (Spirited Scarlet Diamonds, etc.)
  • Healers: Intellect gems
  • Tanks: Stamina gems (don't get cute with other stats)
  • Sockets in gear cost ~100g per slot for gems, and missing even two sockets is like leaving 3-4% damage on the table

Enchants:

  • Weapon: Eternal Intellect (if you haven't enchanted your weapon, uninstall)
  • Chest: Eternal Stats
  • Legs: Temporal Leggings Enchant
  • Boots: Eternal Haste or Speed
  • Gloves: Eternal Dexterity
  • Rings: Eternal Intellect (yes, both rings)

Missing even one enchant is careless. Your guild will notice, and you should feel bad.

Bag Space & Repair

Sounds dumb, but here's the reality check:

  • You need AT LEAST 10-15 free bag slots for consumables, loot, and mana gems
  • If you log in with 2 free slots, go clean out your transmog bags
  • Repair your gear BEFORE raid (ideally by Tuesday evening)
  • Don't be the person who needs repairs 15 minutes in and holds up the raid

Spec Optimization

This is huge and nobody talks about it enough. Your spec isn't just about talents—it's about the entire rotation setup.

Know your talents:

  • Go to wowhead/icy-veins and find the raid-optimal talent build for your class
  • There's usually ONE correct build for Mythic+, ONE for raids
  • If you're "feeling creative" with off-meta talents, you're costing your guild DPS
  • Takes 5 minutes to double-check, so just do it

Stat Priority:

  • Not all secondary stats are created equal
  • Haste? Check the guide. Mastery? Check the guide. Crit? It depends on your spec.
  • Equip your gear in the right priority order, not just "highest item level"

Raid-Specific Mechanics Prep

This sounds like a no-brainer, but I've seen people wiping because they didn't know what their boss does.

Before each raid night:

  • Spend 10 minutes watching the boss mechanics (YouTube guides, dungeon journal)
  • Know what ability requires movement
  • Know if you're a "soak" target or "stack" target
  • Know if your class has a specific interrupt rotation
  • Know the DPS check (if there is one) and whether you're meeting it

For Midnight raid content, the early bosses have tight DPS checks. If you're showing up without consuming potions, you're probably failing the check and wondering why the guild thinks you're undergeared.

Status Buffs (aka Don't Forget World Buffs if They're Active)

Depending on Midnight's ruleset, world buffs might be a thing:

  • Check guild Discord 30 minutes before raid
  • If there's a world buff active, grab it
  • If you miss it, you're running at a disadvantage

Double-Check Your Role Assignment

Know what you're doing before you zone in:

  • Are you main tank, off-tank, or DPS?
  • Are you assigned to add control? Tank swap rotation? Special duty?
  • If you don't know, ask in Discord RIGHT NOW, not in vent 2 minutes before pull

The Reality Check

I've talked to a ton of raiders through the years, and the guilds that clear consistently aren't the ones with the most gear—they're the ones where everyone shows up prepared. It takes maybe 30 minutes to fully prep for a raid night: buying consumables, checking your spec, watching mechanics, fixing gems, getting repairs.

30 minutes of prep saves you from a night of wipes and frustration.
