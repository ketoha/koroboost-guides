Voidspire Raid Guide: All 6 Boss Strategies for Normal and Heroic

A guide by Koroboost

Voidspire is the first raid of Midnight and honestly it's not that scary. The mechanics are telegraphed, the DPS checks are laughable, and most of the "difficulty" comes from people just not paying attention. Here's the breakdown for every boss, normal and heroic.

Pre-Raid Preparation

Before you even walk in, make sure your group has:

  • Minimum 270 ilvl for normal. Seriously, don't show up undergeared.
  • 280+ for heroic. The stat check is real.
  • Flasks and potions. Yeah, they matter on heroic.
  • DBM or Bigwigs installed and configured.

The raid itself is straightforward if your group is reasonably prepared. Most wipes happen because someone didn't read the mechanics, not because the raid is hard.

Boss 1: Ascendant Lord Vladros

Normal: Trivial. This boss exists to teach your group how to move.

Strategy: Spread out when he does his initial cast. The raid takes stacking AoE damage, so stand loosely grouped but not on top of each other. When he marks someone for a big hit, that person moves away. Everyone else stands still and attacks.

The adds that spawn are dangerous only if people stand near them. Kill them if they spawn, ignore them otherwise. Your tank should tell people where to stand.

Heroic: Slightly more aggravating because the damage is higher. Your healer actually has to do things now.

Additional mechanic: Watch for the debuff that reduces your healing taken. When you get it, you need to be more careful about positioning. Don't stand in bad if you're debuffed.

DPS Check: Non-existent. If you can't meet this DPS check, you need to question your rotation.

Difficulty: 1/10. This boss teaches you that raiding isn't hard if people pay attention.

Boss 2: Stonemother Ixen

Normal: Joke boss.

Strategy: The ground disappears in sections. When your section is about to disappear, move to an adjacent section. Don't fall off. Seriously, don't fall off.

When she jumps, move away from where she lands. Her melee hits hard, so ranged stand away, melee stand in position to hit her without taking the jump damage. Your tank positioning is important here.

Heroic: The jumping happens faster and she lands in random spots. You need good awareness.

Additional mechanic: The arena shrinks over time. You'll eventually be stacked in the middle. When this happens, stand at max spread to avoid unnecessary damage, then collapse as space runs out.

The debuff she applies reduces your armor. Tanks will notice their armor dropping. This is okay. Just heal through it.

DPS Check: Not brutal, but the DPS timer is tighter on heroic. If your group is doing less than 30% phase damage, you're in trouble.

Difficulty: 2/10. The jump positioning is the only mechanic anyone actually has to worry about.

Boss 3: Void Ascendant Thex

Normal: This boss is where people's brains usually shut off.

Strategy: Void Ascendant spawns orbs that fly toward random players. Move away from them. Don't let them hit you. If you're marked, run to the side of the room.

The boss has a cast that you must interrupt if possible. Assign interrupts beforehand. If your interrupt rotation is good, you'll never see certain mechanics. This is why assign roles matter.

When Void Ascendant casts his big ability, move to the marked safe zones. The zones are highlighted on the ground. Even bad players can see them. Get in the zone or you die.

Heroic: Orbs move faster. The safe zones are smaller. Interrupts are more important.

Additional mechanic: When the boss is below 50%, he gains a stacking buff that increases his damage. This is why your DPS matters. You can't just casually DPS him down. You need to push phase.

If people get hit by orbs on heroic, they take heavy damage and get a debuff that hurts more. Defensive CDs help here.

DPS Check: Real DPS check on heroic. If your group can't push phase 2 fast enough, the stacking buff kills your healer.

Difficulty: 3/10. The mechanics are straightforward, but people mess up the positioning constantly.

Boss 4: Void Crucible

Normal: Medium difficulty boss that punishes bad play.

Strategy: The Crucible spawns void zones on the ground. Move out of them. They're red. Red bad.

Void Crucible has a pull ability that brings everyone to the center. When he casts this, move to the edges immediately after you're pulled. You'll be safer there.

When void zones stack on top of you, you need to move. Your entire raid needs to coordinate this or you get stacked in a void zone and someone dies.

The boss doesn't do anything mechanically complex. Just move when you need to move and you're fine.

Heroic: Void zones are more aggressive and damage is higher.

Additional mechanic: The boss pulls players toward random void zones instead of toward him. This is a DPS loss but forces better movement.

When debuffed, you trigger void explosions when you move. Walk slowly. Don't panic sprint. This mechanic actually requires thought.

DPS Check: Moderate. You need to push phase before void zones take up the entire arena.

Difficulty: 4/10. This is where casuals start wiping. Movement matters.

Boss 5: Herald of Void

Normal: Getting harder.

Strategy: This boss is all about adds. When herald spawns void adds, kill the priority add first. Your raid leader should call which add to focus.

Herald himself doesn't do much damage. The adds are the threat. They hit melee hard and cast abilities that hurt. If you're standing near an add with a cast, move away.

When Herald casts his big ability, stack up and your healer will heal through it. Don't run away and split the raid.

The DPS check here is real-ish. You need to clear adds while attacking the boss. Split DPS efficiently.

Heroic: Adds spawn faster. Herald hits harder.

Additional mechanic: When Herald reaches certain health thresholds, he gains immunities. You need to focus the adds completely during these phases or you'll get overrun.

The adds themselves are tougher on heroic. Kill them faster or you're dealing with add chaos.

DPS Check: The tightest check so far. You literally cannot fall behind on adds or it cascades into a wipe.

Difficulty: 5/10. This is a DPS check fight. If your DPS can execute, it's a kill.

Boss 6: Void Ascendant Qaz (Final Boss)

Normal: Surprisingly chill for a final boss.

Strategy: Void Ascendant Qaz (okay, they really like the name Ascendant in this raid) has a predictable rotation. Phase 1 he spawns adds and attacks. Kill the adds.

When he casts his big ability, positions matter. Melee stay grouped on the tank. Ranged stand at max range. This spreads out the incoming damage.

At 50% health, he gains a damage buff. This is the DPS check. You need to push through phase 2 before the buff stacks too high.

Phase 2 is identical to phase 1. Kill adds, move for mechanics, don't die.

Heroic: It's the same fight but harder.

Additional mechanic: During phase 2, the boss summons void zones in addition to adds. You're managing both simultaneously. This is chaotic but manageable if your raid is organized.

When mechanics stack, prioritize: Void zones first, then adds, then boss DPS.

The final phase has a soft enrage. The boss gets stronger over time. You need to push him before he becomes unkillable. This is where good DPS shows up.

DPS Check: The biggest check in the raid. Heroic Qaz requires above-average DPS to kill before enrage.

Difficulty: 6/10. This is the only boss that actually requires competent raiders.

Raid-Wide Tips

Stagger cooldowns: Don't use all your defensive CDs on the same ability. Space them out.

Coordinate healing: Your healer needs mana management. People sometimes need to stop taking damage so the healer can drink.

Focus priority: Establish kill order for adds and focus-fire. Communication saves more pulls than skill.

Position pre-fight: Know where you're standing for each mechanic. Don't think about it during the fight.

Use potions: Heroic Qaz especially benefits from damage potions. Push the DPS check.

Healing The Raid

For healers: normal Voidspire is genuinely easy. You have tons of mana and the damage is manageable. Don't be afraid to DPS between heals.

Heroic ramps up the damage significantly. Qaz in particular punishes bad healing. Know your defensive cooldowns and use them proactively, not reactively.

Tanking Notes

Tanking Voidspire is straightforward. Maintain threat, position adds properly, and don't faceplant into mechanics. The tank damage is reasonable. Just don't stand in bad.

DPS Tips

Focus fire the priority target your raid leader calls. Don't tunnel vision the boss while adds take over. Spread when needed, stack when needed, move for mechanics.

Gearing Check

Normal Voidspire (278 ilvl drops): Expect to run it 4-6 times to be reasonably geared.

Heroic Voidspire (285 ilvl drops): Similar grind.

Most guilds split time between normal and heroic based on roster strength. If you're a new guild, normal is the play until you get comfortable.

Final Thoughts

Voidspire is honestly a fun, well-designed raid. The mechanics are fair, the difficulty scales from joke on normal to challenging on heroic, and nothing feels cheap. If your raid can commit to weekly runs, you'll clear it comfortably within the first few weeks of the season.

The biggest thing is getting people to show up prepared and paying attention. That's 90% of raiding right there.

Good luck, kill the bosses, get the loot. You got this.
