Best Arena Comps in WoW Midnight

2v2 and 3v3 meta guide for Season 1. Updated March 2026.

← Back to All Guides

The Midnight arena meta is stabilizing, and if you're pushing rating this season, you're probably asking yourself: should I be playing this comp, or am I gimping myself?

The meta is more forgiving than it's been in years. You can push 2400+ with unconventional teams, but understanding what comps are actually strong gives you the edge you need to climb consistently.

2v2 Arena: S-Tier Comps

Rogue + Warrior

Classic for a reason. Rogue stuns into Warrior burst is still one of the fastest kill rotations in the game.

  • Strengths: Instant burst, chain stuns, mobility from Rogue, cleave from Warrior.
  • Weaknesses: Dies if Rogue gets caught without CDs. Healer comps can kite out their burst.
  • Rating potential: 2600+
Demon Hunter + Any Melee (DH Cleave)

Demon Hunters are cracked in Season 1. The spec has mobility, damage, stuns, and hooks. Play DH with literally any melee dps and you'll win trades.

  • Strengths: Insane single-target damage, Demon Blades procs, death from above setup.
  • Weaknesses: Squishy if caught. No self-healing outside specs.
  • Rating potential: 2500+
Retribution Paladin + Warrior (RET Cleave)

Two melee, both with shields and defensives. This comp is disgusting right now. One goes in, the other supports.

  • Strengths: Both have shields, chain stuns possible, defense rotations carry bad positioning.
  • Weaknesses: Kitable by ranged. Priest can mind control and neutralize both.
  • Rating potential: 2500+

2v2 Arena: A-Tier Comps

Ladder Climbers
  • Mage + Rogue (RMP Classic): Classic for a reason, but harder to execute. Needs team coordination.
  • Death Knight + Warrior: Decent damage, good survivability. Underrated because fewer players practice it.
  • Shadow Priest + Rogue: SP damage is nutty in 2v2. Rogue provides stuns. Works better in 2v2 than 3v3.

3v3 Arena: S-Tier Comps

Rogue + Mage + Healer (RMP)

The gold standard. It's been meta for 15 years for a reason. Every player in the bracket knows this comp exists.

  • Strengths: Rogue CC chain into Mage burst eliminates targets instantly. Healer can sit back and support.
  • Weaknesses: Requires perfect CC coordination. Healer is vulnerable to interrupts.
  • Rating potential: 2800+
DH + Windwalker Monk + Healer (DHak)

Two melee with insane pressure. DH hooks into Windwalker burst is nearly unbeatable.

  • Strengths: Constant pressure from two angles, Windwalker avoids more damage than other melees, DH utility.
  • Weaknesses: Healer is the win condition. If healer dies, you're done.
  • Rating potential: 2700+
Warlock + Any DPS + Healer (Turbo)

Warlock adds pressure the opponent can't ignore. Curses, fears, and damage add up. Hard to counter.

  • Strengths: Warlock pressure forces positioning mistakes, sustain damage, utility.
  • Weaknesses: Warlock is squishy. Warrior cleave into fear creates bad situations.
  • Rating potential: 2600+

3v3 Arena: A-Tier Comps

Ladder Climbers
  • Shadow Priest + Melee + Healer: Priest damage is disgusting. Pairs well with any CC-heavy comp.
  • Ret Paladin + DH + Healer: Two melees with self-healing and shields. Hard to kill anyone.
  • Death Knight + Melee + Healer: DK cooldown management with melee pressure is underrated.

The Healer You Should Play

  • Holy Priest: Best all-rounder. Never a wrong pick. Pain Suppression is insane on anyone.
  • Restoration Druid: More mobile. Better at avoiding melee. Pick if your comp requires kiting.
  • Holy Paladin: If your comp wants to brawl, Paladin sustain is unbeatable.

Meta Weaknesses to Exploit

RMP is everywhere right now. But good Priest teams counter it. DH Cleave is strong, but Healer + ranged DPS can kite forever. Don't play what's meta -- play what counters meta.

Most arena matches aren't won by better gearing or rotations. They're won by the team that doesn't stand in a wall and die to cleave. Learn positioning. It's free rating.

Getting Better at Arena

Watching guides helps, but arena mastery comes from grinding. Play 50 games, watch your replays, and notice what kills you. Adjust. Repeat.
