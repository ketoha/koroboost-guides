Gold in Midnight is weird. The economy shifted hard from Dragonflight because of how professions work now and the catalyst system eating materials like crazy. Lots of the old reliable farms don't work anymore. If you're still running circles in some Dragonflight zone wondering why you're making 2k/hr, time to update your strategy.

I've been tracking income across 3 characters since launch week. Here's what actually works right now.

Tier 1: Crafting Orders (150-250k/week)

Best Gold/Hour

The order system in Midnight got buffed. Public orders actually pay decent gold now because materials are so expensive that people would rather pay a crafter than farm themselves.

Best Crafting Professions for Orders

  • Jewelcrafting -- epic gems sell for 40-60k each depending on your server. One rank 5 gem takes about 10 minutes to craft.
  • Enchanting -- weapon enchants are 8-15k each and literally everyone needs them. Every alt, every respec, every new weapon. Infinite demand.
  • Alchemy -- flasks are 800-1200g each and raiders burn through 20+ per week. Phials are even more expensive.
Pro tip: Don't just sit in the order window waiting. Go to trade chat and advertise your skill rank and specialization. People pay premium for rank 5 crafters.

Tier 3: Mythic+ BoE Farming (50-500k/week)

High Variance

Inconsistent but when it hits, it HITS. Running M+ keys in the 7-10 range drops bind-on-equip items that sell for 30-100k depending on the piece.

The meta right now is speed running lower keys on alts. You're not trying to push rating, you're trying to maximize loot per hour. A coordinated group can clear a +7 in 12-15 minutes.

Best Dungeons for BoE Drops

  • Moonwell Caverns -- high mob density, lots of trash loot
  • Twilight Garrison -- fast clears, decent drop table
  • Dawnbreaker Halls -- two extra packs at the end that most groups skip but they have a separate loot table

If you don't have a group for M+ carries, professional M+ services can run you through keys while you collect the loot.

What NOT to Farm

Dead farms -- skip these:
  • Old raid solos -- legacy gold nerf hit hard. Maybe 5k/hr now. Not worth your time.
  • Fishing -- unless specifically farming feast materials, fish prices crashed week 2.
  • Dragonflight content -- nobody needs these materials anymore. Prices are floor level.
  • World quests for gold -- gold reward scales terribly. 3 WQs for 800g total is insulting.

Quick Tips

  • Install TSM even if you don't flip. The tooltip showing market values saves you from vendoring expensive stuff.
  • Always check region price vs server price before listing. Some items sell better via the cross-realm AH.
  • Don't vendor greens. Disenchant everything. Enchanting materials are expensive this season.
  • Crafting alts are worth the investment. Level one JC alt and it pays for itself in 3 days.
  • Check Wowhead economy section weekly for material price trends.
