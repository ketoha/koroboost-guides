Mythic+ Dungeon Guide

WoW Midnight Season 1 - All 8 dungeons covered. Last updated March 2026.

Midnight Season 1 features eight dungeons split between the Silvermoon/Ghostlands zones and returning classics with a Midnight twist. The dungeon pool is harder than The War Within Season 4 on average, with tighter timers and more dangerous trash. Here is a quick-reference guide for every dungeon based on our experience running Mythic+ carries at every key level.

Season 1 Affix: Void Corruption

Starting at +12, Void Corruption activates. Every 3 seconds, the entire party takes shadow damage equal to 2% of their max HP. This damage ignores armor but not magic damage reduction. It cannot be dispelled or outranged. The damage scales with key level: at +15, it is roughly 45K per tick for a DPS with 2.2M HP. Healers need to account for an additional 15K HPS per person just from this affix alone. It does not sound like much, but over a 30-minute dungeon, it adds up to millions of extra healing required.

Dungeon Breakdown

Magisters' Terrace (Reimagined) Hard

The hardest dungeon in the pool. The trash corridor leading to the second boss (Sunfury Archmage Velanis) has back-to-back packs of Sunfury Magisters that chain-cast Arcane Volley. If even one goes off, it hits the group for 1.8M. You need to chain interrupts and bring AoE stuns. The final boss, Kael'thas (echo version), does a Pyroblast that must be interrupted on the 3rd cast or it one-shots the tank. Timer is 33 minutes and most groups struggle to beat 35.

Sunstrider Crypts Hard

An underground dungeon beneath the royal crypts of Silvermoon. The Crypt Stalker packs do a frontal knockback that chains into the next pack if you are not positioned against a wall. The second boss, Bonecaller Zul'rath, summons skeletons every 20 seconds that fixate random players. If a skeleton reaches its target, it explodes for 2M. DPS need to swap immediately. The gauntlet before the final boss requires the group to run through collapsing tunnels while dodging void zones. This is where most keys are lost.

Dawnstar Spire Medium

A vertical dungeon with an elevator mechanic between floors. The Arcane Sentinels on the second floor cast a reflectable spell that does 3M damage. Spell Reflection or grounding effects trivialize these pulls. The elevator boss (Sunsworn Commander Tyralion) fights on a small circular platform. If you get knocked off, you die. The final boss, Archmage Aethas Sunreaver, phases at 30% and teleports the group to a shadow realm where you fight adds for 45 seconds. Timer is 30 minutes.

Thalassian Depths Medium

An underwater/coastal dungeon in the ruined harbors east of Silvermoon. Movement speed is reduced by 30% in the submerged sections (about 40% of the dungeon). Druid Stampeding Roar and personal movement abilities are critical. The first boss, Tidecaller Nyn'sera, does a whirlpool mechanic that pulls players toward the center. Sprint or blink out or you take 1.5M and get stunned. Trash is mostly caster mobs that need kicks. Good dungeon for ranged-heavy groups.

Elrendar Crossing Easy

The easiest dungeon in the pool. It is an outdoor dungeon along the Elrendar River between Eversong Woods and the Ghostlands. The river sections slow by 50%, but you can largely avoid them with proper routing. Trash is straightforward melee mobs with a few casters mixed in. The bosses have simple mechanics. Moongraze Matriarch (first boss) does a charge at a random player, which is easily sidestepped. Most groups time this comfortably even on +15. Timer is 28 minutes.

Ghostlands Bastion Medium

A Scourge-themed dungeon in the Dead Scar area. Undead trash mobs apply a healing absorption debuff that stacks. Healers need to power through the absorb before actual healing kicks in. The second boss, Deathstalker Reva, goes into stealth at 50% and ambushes a random player for 2.2M physical damage. Defensive cooldowns or externals are mandatory. The dungeon is linear with no real routing decisions, which makes it consistent.

Void Terrace Hard

A brand-new dungeon set in a void-corrupted section of Silvermoon. Everything here hits like a truck. The trash does pulsing shadow AoE that increases in damage the longer you stay in combat. You have to pull fast and kill faster. The second boss, Void Harbinger Xal'mora, opens void portals that spawn adds every 15 seconds. If you do not kill the boss before the 4th portal, you get overwhelmed. This is a DPS check boss. The final boss is a two-phase fight with a 6-minute hard enrage. Groups that cannot push 12M group DPS will not make it.

Farstrider Lodge Easy

A nature-themed dungeon in Eversong Woods. Mostly beast-type mobs that are vulnerable to crowd control. Hibernate, Freezing Trap, and Polymorph all work here, giving you flexible pull options. The bosses are forgiving with telegraphed mechanics. Ranger-Captain Areiel (final boss) does a Hunter's Mark on a random player followed by a kill shot 5 seconds later. The marked player needs to line-of-sight behind a pillar. Fun, straightforward dungeon that most groups enjoy.

Route Priority for Key Pushing

  1. Get comfortable with Elrendar Crossing and Farstrider Lodge first. These are your free +15 timings.
  2. Learn Dawnstar Spire and Ghostlands Bastion next. Medium difficulty with consistent timers.
  3. Thalassian Depths requires a specific group comp (movement-heavy) but is not mechanically hard.
  4. Magisters' Terrace, Sunstrider Crypts, and Void Terrace are the wall dungeons. Practice on +10-12 before attempting +15.

General M+ Tips

  • Bring a Bloodlust/Heroism for every run. Do not rely on drums. The 5% difference matters at +15.
  • Potions of Void Resistance reduce the Void Corruption affix damage by 20%. Use them on hard trash pulls.
  • Communication on interrupts is the #1 factor in timing keys. Set up a kick order before the key starts.
  • Route add-ons (MDT) have not been fully updated for Midnight pathing yet. Check community routes on Raider.IO weekly.
